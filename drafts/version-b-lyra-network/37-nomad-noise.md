# Chapter 37: Nomad Noise

Grim heard the noise first and refused to call it a message.

"Messages imply sender intent, receiver model, encoding, and responsibility," he said. "This is correlation behavior with delusions of grammar."

"So, a committee," Lars said.

"Worse. A universe-adjacent committee."

They were three jumps from the dead portal, running quiet along the KBC Void edge. Havets carried passive instruments older than most governments. Fastulv carried Aphex-certified survey junk to satisfy anyone looking. Between them, Grim had built a detector array from Creator map corrections, Mira's fatigue models, Kesh's tolerances, and a NUKEA reactor diagnostic that had not consented to becoming cosmology but performed well under protest.

The noise appeared only when no one looked straight at it.

That was not mysticism. It was measurement interaction. High-energy scans collapsed the correlations into thermal nonsense. Low-power passive watching let patterns persist across instruments separated by impossible timing. Not faster-than-light signaling in the usable sense. More like field states agreeing that they had been disturbed by the same absent hand.

Mira loved and hated it. "It is not saying words."

"Correct," Grim said.

"It is maintaining tolerances across nonlocal anchors."

"Possibly."

"Possibly is doing heroic labor."

"I learned from you."

The name Nomad came from Lyra's later notes, recovered in fragments from Arkology Zero. Not a species name, maybe not even a civilization's own category. Multiverse Nomads: field-like intelligences, or post-biological networks, or old engineers who had reduced themselves to communication patterns because bodies were too loud across deep time.

No gods.

Just old engineers, perhaps, who had solved survival by becoming less centrally killable than stars.

The first intelligible event occurred when Fabron, bored and irritated, recited a Clear Hand maintenance prayer under his breath while repairing a loose sensor bracket.

The noise changed.

Not to the prayer. To the timing of his repair: touch, test, release, wait, retest. The old ritual encoded a safe interaction pattern with uncertain systems. The Nomad field, if field was the right insult, responded to the practice rather than the words.

Grim froze the data.

"Again," he said.

Fabron looked offended. "I am not a laboratory animal."

"No. You are a culturally preserved interface protocol."

"That is worse."

Lars laughed, then stopped when the noise produced a local thermal dip in the shape of a handprint on Havets' inner hull.

Five fingers.

Too small.

Lyra's little hands are in the walls.

The handprint vanished, leaving unstable nanomaterial residue with isotopic ratios no local stock could explain.

Valdrun whispered, "Lyra."

Lars did not answer. The ache in him was not a migraine, not a lock, not proof. It was wanting.

Mira collected the residue before anyone could turn longing into doctrine.

"Evidence first," she said gently.

For once, no one argued.
