# Chapter 49: Havets Sista Sång Sings

Havets Sista Sång had been built for wars no current navy had vocabulary to describe.

Lars had used it like a hidden knife, a ghost in fold-space, a patient advantage. He had not understood until the mass event how much of the old ship's power was not in weapons but in timing.

Black Hole launched against nine sites and twelve decoys within the same hour.

Harvest craft moved through brown-dwarf wakes, medical traffic, refugee lanes, and Syren pilgrimage routes. Some targeted hidden Aerits. Some targeted ordinary people misidentified by corrupted models. Some targeted Lyra's planted bait: empty apartments, preserved-body vaults already evacuated, false medical archives designed to bleed predators of confidence.

No central command could answer fast enough.

So there was none.

Havets sang a timing lattice across the network.

Not orders. Windows.

A fold shadow opened for a farm convoy exactly when its pursuers lost sensor lock. A reactor guild received a harmless diagnostic tone that meant switch to manual. Fabron's order heard an old hymn cadence in emergency static and moved shelter families before the official alert. Kesh's archive kin-circle delayed a legal hearing by eleven minutes, enough for an aquatic linguist to choose evacuation without identity disclosure. Mira saw stress in L-Prime's outer ring and identified a decoy harvest hull before Aphex weapons wasted fire on the wrong target.

Fastulv did the visible work. It appeared in three places it could not plausibly be, thanks to drones, spoofed engine profiles, and Lars's willingness to let everyone believe his freighter was held together by crime and spite.

Grim coordinated uncertainty. Every packet carried confidence levels and refusal options. No one had to obey a mystery signal. Many did because the signal matched practices they already trusted.

At Mirrorglass, Sella Korr rejected evacuation, then accepted relocation for twelve children and two sick crew because the offer did not demand she become special. She stayed in her algae tower and vented nutrient fog through a maintenance shaft, blinding a boarding drone long enough for local security to foam it.

"Coherence enough," she sent.

At the shipyard clinic, the partially awakened medic identified infected blood filters and saved thirty technicians without remembering his old name.

At the Tau Ceti archive, Kesh's people invoked kin-law and made Black Hole argue with procedure until Havets folded a pursuing craft into the path of a regulatory beacon.

Havets fired only twice.

Both times, it disabled engines.

That restraint cost them one shelter node. Black Hole hit it with kinetic debris after missing the evacuees. Fabron's youngest Clear Hand brother died sealing a manual hatch from the outside.

His name was Luc Moreau. He had known almost nothing. He had carried tools, tea, and a paper list of frightened families.

After the hour ended, the network still stood.

Black Hole's mass acquisition event failed as a system, though not without blood.

Havets went quiet.

Lars stood in its command chamber and felt the old ship's fatigue like a weather front.

"You sang well," he said.

The ship answered with a maintenance list so long Grim made a distressed sound.

Even ancient miracles needed repair.
