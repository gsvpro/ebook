# Chapter 45: The Cancer Model

Grim modeled galactic collapse as cancer because Lars had insulted three cleaner metaphors.

"Cancer is uncontrolled replication with local success and systemic failure," Grim said. "Black Hole B-7, Syren spectacle, Aphex monopoly temptation, emergency governments, artifact cults, even heroic rescue when centralized around you: all display malignant scaling under fear."

"I dislike being in the same category as tumors."

"Then stop sharing growth behavior."

The model filled Havets' chamber. Red patterns showed acquisition: blood markets, data theft, biological nurseries, cult recruitment, procurement capture. Blue patterns showed Återviens response: refusal, shelter, repair, whistleblowing, logistics, consent ledgers, local autonomy. Gold showed unknown Nomad/Creator interactions, deliberately under-modeled to prevent false certainty.

Mira adjusted thresholds. "Black Hole does not need to understand Aerit nanotech. It only needs to create enough partial compatibility to sell fear and weapons."

Fabron added, "Syren does not need truth. It needs bodies onto which truth-shaped grief can be projected."

Vigor pointed with a spoon. "Aphex does not need evil. It needs efficiency rewarded until every local spare part becomes a subscription."

Arsenault, projected in miniature, looked wounded. "I am present."

"We know," Vigor said.

The cancer model changed strategy. They stopped asking how to defeat Black Hole in one battle and asked how to reduce its nutrient supply. Blood markets needed buyers, labs, transport, corrupt clinics, mythic demand, and victims isolated enough to vanish. Cut one and the tumor adapted. Cut many locally and it starved.

The network prepared a distributed immune response.

Farmers would shelter families under seasonal labor movements. Reactor guilds would flag biological shipping anomalies as coolant risks. Aphex dissenters would leak procurement routes to jurisdictions that hated Arsenault just enough to act. NUKEA recalls would seed privacy servers. Fabron's orders would preach against sacred availability without naming Aerits. Alien kin-circles would enforce consent law around suspected long-lived persons. Grim would coordinate models but publish uncertainty with every recommendation.

"No command center," Lars said.

Grim looked at him. "You are saying that to yourself."

"Yes. Is it working?"

"Intermittently."

Lyra's triage index contained one terrifying update: Black Hole was preparing a mass acquisition event across at least nine suspected Aerit sites and several false ones. Some sites held hidden Aerits. Some held ordinary people misidentified by bad models. Some held bait planted by Lyra's apparatus decades earlier.

"Decades," Fabron said softly. "She expected this?"

Valdrun answered, "She expected appetite. Details are weather."

The final model did not promise victory. It promised load reduction, containment, and survivable adaptation over ninety, three hundred, and seven hundred years.

Lars stared at those horizons until his own lifespan felt less like tragedy and more like assignment.

"All right," he said. "We do not kill the cancer with one heroic knife."

Vigor nodded.

"We teach the body to refuse it."
