# Chapter 48: The Public Lie

The galaxy learned that Black Hole B-7 had operated an illegal longevity trafficking ring.

That was true.

It did not learn that some blood samples belonged to hidden Aerits, that Lyra's recovery apparatus had seeded false trails for decades, that Creator portals sat atop older Nomad anchors, or that several people in the scandal were older than their grandparents' nations.

That was also deliberate.

Lars hated the press conference more than the firefight.

Arsenault stood before cameras with the expression of a man nobly enduring consequences he had already priced. Beside him stood regulators, alien advocates, Homa Sen, and a public health minister who had learned three hours earlier that her predecessor owned stock in a clinic chain now under quarantine. Fabron watched from security. Vigor watched from a food truck outside, feeding protesters and journalists with equal aggression.

Lars did not stand on stage.

Pell of Humanity Ascendant called that proof of guilt. Syren channels called it proof of sacred concealment. Black Hole called for revenge. Everyone wanted the missing center.

The network gave them a smaller story with enough truth to hold.

Stolen biological material.

Illegal cloning scaffolds.

Procurement corruption.

Medical consent fraud.

Corporate negligence.

Paramilitary trafficking.

No ancient blood. No sleepers. No gods. No chosen savior.

Mira watched the broadcast from L-Prime, arms folded. "This lie protects me."

"Yes," Lars said.

"And many others."

"Yes."

"And lets some deeper truths remain unaccountable."

He did not dodge. "Yes."

She nodded slowly. "Then document the lie where future repair can find it."

So Grim built a sealed ledger: what they hid, why, who consented, who could challenge it, and when review would reopen. Not a central registry of hidden Aerits. A moral debt ledger. Stored in shards across Arkology Zero, Kesh's archive, Mother Aveline's paper vault, Vigor's seed bank, and one Aphex escrow Arsenault could not alter without destroying evidence against himself.

"I dislike being part of my own leash," Arsenault said.

"Personal growth is awful," Lars replied.

The public lie held for six days.

That was enough.

Victims' families found bodies and names. Clinics closed. Buyers fled into legal nets. Black Hole lost funders who preferred evil without subpoenas. Syren lost some momentum when Homa Sen kept repeating, "A person is not evidence you can own," on every channel that would suffer her.

Pell adapted, of course. Predators and politicians shared that insult. But his story had to bend around documented crimes now, and bending slowed it.

At night, Lars watched unedited footage of Cryo Vault Three. He saw sample cases, shaking hands, Mira refusing to cry until the chain of custody was complete.

"Necessary lie?" Grim asked.

"Temporary lie," Lars said. "If it becomes permanent, it becomes rot."

Grim entered that into the ledger without being asked.
