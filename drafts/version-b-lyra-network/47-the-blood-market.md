# Chapter 47: The Blood Market

The blood market hid inside a luxury medical exchange where rich people bought years by pretending vocabulary made extraction ethical.

It occupied a private moonlet with gardens under glass, surgical suites cleaner than conscience, and contracts written to make consent look transferable. Black Hole used the exchange as a buyer channel, laundering stolen biological material through longevity clinics, military research fronts, and Syren-adjacent relic funds.

Lars wanted to burn it.

Homa Sen, reviewing the plan, said, "Fire destroys records. Records hang owners."

So they brought auditors.

Not Aphex auditors. Worse.

Insurance auditors, bioethics regulators, alien kin-law advocates, tax investigators, customs officers, and one retired food safety inspector Vigor described as "a woman who can smell fraud through marble." Fabron's soldiers protected the teams. Grim corrupted appointment systems so every executive arrived at once. Fastulv docked as a waste contractor. Havets waited in a fold seam with quiet knives.

Mira came in person because the exchange's material storage used composite cryo-racks, and she trusted cracks more than people.

The first hour was bureaucracy.

The second was panic.

By the third, Black Hole guards revealed themselves by trying to evacuate samples through a chapel spa. Fabron's squad pinned them with foam and ceramic nets while the food safety inspector blocked a side door with a sanitation cart and language that impressed Lars.

In Cryo Vault Three, Mira found the blood.

Not vials. Libraries. Human, alien, altered, partial, mislabeled. Some ordinary. Some from suspected Aerits. Some from people Black Hole had only hoped were valuable and killed anyway.

Her face went white.

"Chain of custody," she said.

Lars heard the effort in it. Rage wanted spectacle. Evidence wanted hands that did not shake.

They cataloged. They sealed. They copied ownership chains to jurisdictions that hated one another too much to cooperate in a cover-up. Arsenault leaked Aphex buyer contacts before Lars could threaten him, which was either growth or strategy and probably both.

Then Black Hole triggered the disposal burn.

Cryo Vault Three began heating from the floor.

Mira read the rack stress in one glance. "Twenty minutes before breach. Less if we move wrong."

Havets could fold the vault out. Maybe. Doing so would reveal capabilities and risk sample decoherence. Lars opened the command channel, felt the old ship ready itself, and stopped.

"Local first," he said.

Mira smiled without humor. "Good. Help me steal a moon."

They did not steal the moon. They stole its thermal path. NUKEA emergency kits, planted through recall channels, rerouted coolant. Vigor's waste contractor containers accepted cryo-racks. Fabron carried sample cases while swearing at rich architecture. Grim held three failing doors closed with his body and later complained one had scratched his finish.

When Black Hole reinforcements arrived, Havets finally sang.

A fold-shear passed through their drives and left them drifting, alive, powerless, and surrounded by regulatory ships with excellent warrants.

The blood market did not burn.

It became evidence.

For predators, that was worse.
