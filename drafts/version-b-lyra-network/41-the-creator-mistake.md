# Chapter 41: The Creator Mistake

The Creators had been brilliant enough to be dangerous and wrong enough to vanish.

That was Mira's summary after seven days with the map, the dead portal data, and Kesh's tolerances. She delivered it in Havets' analysis chamber while eating Vigor's flatbread over a sensor console until Grim threatened a cleanliness protocol.

"They found the Nomad anchor network," Mira said. "They recognized stable correlations across distance and built transport machinery to exploit them. But the anchor tolerances were not designed for matter throughput. Every portal use created semantic noise in a system that may have been carrying identity-state coherence."

Fabron looked pained. "They drove trains through a nervous system."

"Crude," Grim said. "But useful."

Lars stared at the Creator failure overlays. Entire portal clusters showed escalating correction demands, then shutdown, then silence. Not explosion. Not war, at least not in the available data. Collapse by feedback, misuse, and perhaps guilt if they had been capable of it.

Valdrun's voice was low. "The Creators may have broken what they did not understand."

"Or discovered they were breaking it and stopped too late," Mira said.

The distinction mattered. Villains were easy. Failed engineers were kin in a terrible way.

A hidden sublayer in Lyra's bad-body journal matched the new model. She had suspected the portal network was a parasitic adaptation on older communication anchors. Her recovery apparatus avoided Creator doors except as last resort because every forced transit increased local noise around hidden Aerit locks.

"Bad locks on purpose," Lars said.

The phrase from the dead subway returned.

Grim unpacked the implication. "Lyra may have accepted imperfect memory locks because perfect locks would require central synchronization. Bad locks cause individual suffering and local leakage, but they fail independently."

Mira went very still.

"So my missing life is a design tradeoff."

No one answered quickly enough.

Lars did, because delay would be cowardice. "Maybe."

"That is monstrous."

"Yes."

"And possibly necessary."

"Also yes."

She left the chamber for an hour.

No one followed. Consent applied to grief too.

When she returned, her face was composed in a way Lars distrusted because he recognized it.

"I still do not consent to unlocking," she said.

"Understood."

"But I consent to helping model the locks as engineering, not destiny. If they are bad, we can learn their failure modes. If they were tradeoffs, we can choose better ones now."

Valdrun bowed her head.

Fabron murmured, "Repair before worship."

Mira pointed at him. "Do not make this liturgical."

"I would not dare."

"You absolutely would."

The room breathed again.

The Creator mistake became a warning inside the network: do not confuse access with understanding, or scale with wisdom, or survival with permission.

Black Hole would never learn that. Syren would turn it into a hymn. Aphex would patent the wrong half if unwatched.

Återviens would have to do something harder.

Maintain the unknown without owning it.
