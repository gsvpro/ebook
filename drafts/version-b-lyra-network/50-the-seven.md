# Chapter 50: The Seven

They did not gather in a circle.

That would have been too symbolic and too easy to target. The seven guardians aligned through delays, proxies, dead drops, quantum-noise windows, paper messages, maintenance dust, and one insulting recipe correction from Ruun that made Vigor threaten an ancient saboteur with a spoon.

Valdrun represented patience.

Ödesvaka represented protocol.

Ruun represented distributed mischief and arrived as flicker in three light fixtures.

Two guardians remained unnamed by mutual safety, one speaking through an asteroid-mining union's safety manual, the other through a preserved-body agent that could maintain projection for only ninety seconds before its knees failed.

The sixth sent only models.

The seventh was Lars, which everyone treated as unfortunate but currently factual.

The agenda was limited activation.

Black Hole had failed to harvest at scale, but it still held victims, labs, and enough stolen data to remain lethal. Syren had not collapsed. Pell had not disappeared. The Creator/Nomad mystery had opened larger risks. Lyra's apparatus had revealed itself enough to become vulnerable if mishandled.

"We need capacity," Lars said.

Valdrun answered, "We need restraint with capacity."

Ödesvaka displayed the protocol from Chapter 46, now revised after the mass event. Limited activation meant no awakening wave. It meant regional consent teams, local shelters, privacy infrastructure, and three classes of Aerit contact: threat evidence only, procedural partial, and identity restoration under extended review.

Ruun's light flicker formed words on the wall.

Also sabotage. Do not forget sabotage. Sabotage is repair applied to enemies.

Fabron, present as human liaison, murmured, "I like that one."

"You would," Lars said.

The debate lasted nine hours. Guardians thought in decades and centuries. Lars kept trying to shorten timelines because people were bleeding now. The others kept lengthening them because civilizations bled longer when frightened men built emergency thrones.

In the end, they authorized three hundred years of apparatus continuity planning, ninety years of near-term recovery support, and immediate operations against remaining Black Hole nurseries.

They also passed a rule aimed so obviously at Lars that even Grim looked sympathetic.

No guardian may become sole interpreter of Lyra's apparatus.

Lars voted yes.

It felt like cutting off an escape route he had secretly wanted.

Afterward, the preserved-body agent turned toward him. Its face was unfinished, genderless by failure rather than design, skin shimmering at the edges.

"Lyra trusted you to refuse being trusted too much," it said.

"That sounds like her being annoying from beyond logistics."

"Yes."

The agent collapsed into gray residue. Grim collected it with reverence and a vacuum wand.

The seven had aligned.

Not united. Not restored. Not ready to rule.

Aligned enough.
