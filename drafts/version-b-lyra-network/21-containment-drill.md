# Chapter 21: Containment Drill

The three ships came in through the brown dwarf's particle wake like knives dragged through smoke.

Mira saw them before Aphex tactical did. The fatigue sensors around Materials Ring B were designed to watch test hulls, not attackers, but stress did not care about intention. Hulls flexed. Heat seams complained. Compensation fields tried to hide load, and Mira read the hiding as clearly as the load itself.

"Not built," she said. "Grown around stolen frames. One human-pattern cutter inside each shell. The rest is tissue and black particulate."

Fabron's mouth tightened. "Boarding craft."

"Harvest craft," Lars corrected.

He stood in the command annex Marchand had surrendered to him after the third time he used board authority like a crowbar. Fastulv remained docked at Dock Two, loud, visible, and suddenly broadcasting panicked engine diagnostics in six incompatible formats. It looked like a freighter about to die of maintenance debt.

That was deliberate.

Havets Sista Sång did not appear on any screen. Its presence arrived as absences: a missing radar reflection, a clean gap in radiation clutter, a fold-shadow where the brown dwarf's gravity should have made clean geometry impossible. Lars felt its command lattice at the back of his mind, old and patient, waiting for a request rather than a throne.

He did not get the old lock-migraine anymore. That mercy had consequences. The pressure behind his eyes now was ordinary fear, memory resonance, and the knowledge that he could do too much if he forgot to ask smaller questions.

"Containment drill," Lars said on the station-wide channel. "All departments execute manual isolation. No remote reopen. No heroics. If a voice you know gives a bad order, disobey it politely."

Fabron looked at him. "Politely?"

"Your people are French-adjacent. They need cultural permission."

"I will shoot you after this."

"Queue forms."

The first harvest craft fired a docking spike at Habitation Three. Fastulv woke with theatrical indignation, kicked its clamps, and vented a false plasma bloom across Dock Two. Aphex targeting systems screamed about a freighter reactor breach. The harvest craft corrected toward the easier prey.

Havets moved.

The old ship did not fire a weapon anyone could see. It folded a sliver of wake turbulence into the attackers' own compensation fields. Mira watched the stress signatures invert. One grown hull tried to stiffen against a load that no longer existed and cracked along a tendon seam.

"Beautiful," she whispered, then hated that she had.

The second craft launched drones. Grim caught half in station security spoofing and made them believe the nearest valid target was an executive chapel module. They ate the chapel's gold leaf, found no blood, and died in containment foam.

The third craft was smarter. It ignored Fastulv, ignored the chapel, and burned straight for the med bay where the captured hostile lay under guard.

Mira's fingers flew across the fatigue console. "It wants its specimen back. Or wants to kill it."

"Both are rude," Lars said.

Fabron had already moved. He took Mbeki's squad down the emergency ladder because lifts could be lied to. Lars wanted to go after him. Instead he stayed where he could coordinate and hated every responsible second of it.

The third craft breached an external maintenance blister. The station shook. No migraine came. Only memory: Lyra on Pluto, bleeding into cold regolith, saying redundancy before she had the word.

Lars opened a narrow command request to Havets. Not attack. Not rescue. Constraint.

Havets answered with ancient satisfaction.

The breach corridor collapsed around the boarders in three neat segments. Fabron's squad met the survivors at the manual hatch with foam rounds, ceramic nets, and disciplined terror. Mira fed them stress maps in real time, calling out armor seams like she was reading cracked beams.

"Left hip. Neck ring. Do not hit the chest, it vents particulate."

Fabron obeyed without arguing, which proved the universe had become strange.

When it was over, two attackers were dust, one was sealed, and Habitation Three had lost atmosphere in an empty storage bay because a night-shift maintenance crew had ignored Aphex design and installed an illegal manual bulkhead.

On the bulkhead's inner face, under fresh frost, someone had painted three interlocking circles and a cracked line left unrepaired.

Fabron saw it. So did Lars.

Mira saw them seeing it.

"That symbol," she said.

Fabron answered too quickly. "Maintenance superstition."

Lars looked through the scarred viewport at the wounded brown dwarf facility and the glittering remains drifting beyond it.

"No," he said. "It's older than superstition."

Mira's hand rested on the sensor board. Her shoulder trembled from pain, shock, and something under both.

"Evidence first," she said.

Lars nodded.

"Evidence first."
