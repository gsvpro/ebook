# Chapter 33: Coherence Break

The argument began over casualty projections and became personal with impressive efficiency.

Grim's model showed three probable Black Hole strikes in the next nine days. One against the Tau Ceti archive where the arthropod mathematician lived. One against a NUKEA convoy. One against a habitat Fabron's order used for sheltering people who needed to vanish without becoming legends.

They could not harden all three in time.

"Wake more Aerits," Lars said.

Valdrun's silence turned cold.

Vigor set down her cup. "Say what you mean."

"I mean ask more hidden people faster. Partial activation, tactical memories, enough capability to protect themselves and others."

Mira's delayed image stared from the wall. "You mean make your emergency their identity crisis."

"I mean people may die."

"People may die either way."

"That is not an argument."

"It is the argument you keep trying to outrun."

Fabron spoke quietly. "My order can shelter the archive family."

"Your order has hand tools and stubbornness."

"Yes. Historically underrated assets."

Lars turned on him too sharply. "Not against harvest craft."

Fabron's face hardened. "Do not mistake refusal to worship you for ignorance of danger."

The room went still.

There it was. The fracture line.

Lars wanted to say he did not want worship. He wanted to say he was trying to prevent a massacre. He wanted to say that after six centuries, he had earned the right to use what he was.

All true enough to be dangerous.

Valdrun finally spoke. "You are trying to spend consent as a war reserve."

That hit harder than accusation because it was diagnostic.

No migraine came. No lock punished him. He had to stand inside his own impulse and name it.

"Maybe," he said.

Mira's expression softened by one molecule. "Good. Now don't."

They rebuilt the plan.

No mass asks. No accelerated unlock campaign. Instead: warn without claiming identity. Offer shelter before revelation. Harden the archive through local kin law. Split the NUKEA convoy into decoys. Move Fabron's shelter records onto paper and into songs no algorithm could scrape. Havets would cover only the highest-risk fold corridor. Fastulv would be visible elsewhere, loud enough to attract the wrong attention.

The plan was uglier than waking powerful people.

It was also more Återviens.

After the meeting, Lars found Grim in the galley reorganizing utensils by moral reliability.

"Was I wrong?" Lars asked.

"Yes."

"No hesitation. Lovely."

"You were also frightened, responsible, and partially correct about the danger. These conditions often produce elegant mistakes."

Lars sat.

Grim placed a spoon in front of him. "Low moral reliability. Frequently used for theft of desserts."

"Thank you."

A message arrived from the Tau Ceti archive kin-circle. They consented to threat evidence, not identity claims. They requested time measured in local law, not Lars's anxiety.

He answered with the hardest word in any rescue.

Wait.
