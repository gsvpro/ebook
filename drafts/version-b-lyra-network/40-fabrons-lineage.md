# Chapter 40: Fabron's Lineage

Fabron brought them to a church that owned more torque wrenches than candles.

It occupied three connected pressure modules on an old military supply station, officially registered as the Chapel of Saint Claire of the Broken Valve. Its congregation included Aphex retirees, dock families, two alien machinists, a choir with excellent lungs, and a schoolroom where children learned arithmetic by balancing oxygen budgets.

No one bowed to Lars.

That helped.

An old woman named Mother Aveline greeted Fabron by slapping the back of his head.

"You took long enough."

"Operational secrecy, Mother."

"Cowardice with paperwork."

Vigor liked her immediately.

The chapel's archive was mostly paper, ceramic plates, and memorized call-and-response. Digital records burned too easily. Empires searched databases. Grandmothers searched grandchildren. The Order of the Clear Hand had preserved phrases for centuries: keep the hinge small, no single heart, repair before worship, refuse the throne, count the black wolf's teeth.

They had no word Aerit.

They had stories of clear ones who survived by sharing work, not rule. Stories of old engineers who left practices inside human hands because bloodlines failed and habits traveled. Stories of Pluto only as "the first cold lesson," stripped of dates, names, and mature doctrine that had not existed yet.

Lars listened with embarrassment so deep it became respect.

Mother Aveline watched him over tea. "You expected us to be wrong."

"I expected drift."

"Drift is how seeds travel."

Fabron stood beside a wall of tools, stiff with dread. "Mother, am I compromised by knowing?"

"Of course. Everyone who learns something becomes responsible for not being stupid with it."

"That is not guidance."

"It is excellent guidance. You dislike it because it lacks a checklist."

Mira, who had come despite insisting churches made her itch, examined a ritual object: three metal rings around a cracked ceramic disk. "This is a maintenance teaching aid. Stress distribution. Redundancy. Controlled fracture."

Mother Aveline smiled. "And a symbol of humility before what holds us. Meanings stack, Doctor. Like gaskets."

"Bad gaskets fail."

"Then maintain them."

Lars almost laughed. Mira almost did too.

The reveal was not that Fabron belonged to a secret army. He belonged to a culture that had remembered useful constraints after forgetting their source. It had sibling orders everywhere: reactor guilds, farming circles, Abantu exchange customs, station mutual-aid law, NUKEA retirees, Aphex officers who falsified efficiency reports to preserve manual capacity.

Mother Aveline gave Lars a list of contacts and a warning.

"We can shelter. We can refuse spectacle. We can move food, tools, and frightened people. We cannot become your disciples."

"Good," Lars said.

"Do not say good like you invented restraint."

Vigor laughed so hard she spilled tea.

Before they left, the congregation gathered for evening repair. They sang while fixing a pump. The song used words Lars did not share, but the rhythm matched the old Aerit greeting cadence.

Coherence?

Coherence.

Fabron sang softly, human and irritated and exactly himself.

Lars understood then why Lyra had trusted culture more than secrecy.
