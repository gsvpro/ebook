# Chapter 46: Wake No More Than Needed

The hardest vote was not whether to fight.

It was how many sleeping people to disturb before the fight reached them.

Lyra's triage index listed hundreds of likely hidden Aerits within Black Hole's widened acquisition cone. Each entry carried probabilities, not labels: threat exposure, local resilience, memory-lock fragility, consent access, social dependency, century-scale risk if awakened, century-scale risk if left alone.

Lars hated every column.

The seven guardians did not all gather in one room. That would have been tactically obscene. Valdrun joined from Havets. Ödesvaka from Arkology Zero. Ruun through jittering maintenance dust recovered near the nursery. Two others answered as delayed protocols from deep routes, identities masked even from Lars. One remained silent except for a single coherence pulse that made Havets' old hull ring like a bell.

Coherence uncertain, the pulse meant.

Honest, at least.

Mira attended as herself, not as an Aerit representative. Sella Korr sent a refusal statement from Mirrorglass that began, I am still not your reserve army, and became required reading. Kesh's kin-circle sent a legal framework for threat evidence without identity capture. Fabron's order sent Mother Aveline, who brought tea and contempt for grandiosity.

The protocol they forged was narrow.

Wake no more than needed.

Ask before identity.

Protect before ask when danger is immediate, but do not convert protection into debt.

Partial unlock only with recorded boundaries and independent stop authority.

No public list.

No central registry.

No guardian may override refusal alone.

If emergency breach forces action without consent, the act must be logged as harm, reviewed as harm, and repaired as harm. Survival would not launder violation into virtue.

Lars signed last.

Not because he disagreed. Because his hand shook.

Valdrun noticed. "Good."

"That is your response to visible distress?"

"A steady hand signing this would worry me."

They authorized three immediate asks.

One to a shipyard medic whose clinic was already under Black Hole surveillance.

One to an aquatic linguist whose kin-circle had requested threat evidence.

One to Mira, if and only if she chose to change her boundary.

Mira looked at her copy for a long time.

"Not today," she said.

"Understood," Lars answered.

This time disappointment arrived smaller. Discipline had become less theatrical with practice.

The shipyard medic said yes to partial procedural memory and woke with the ability to identify Black Hole particulate infection in blood filters. The aquatic linguist said yes to threat evidence and no to unlocking, then mobilized three kin-networks faster than any guardian could have. Mira stayed Mira and improved the lock fragility model by fourteen percent.

Three different answers.

Three valid rescues.

Black Hole's mass event countdown continued.

But the network entered it without conscripting its own sleepers.
