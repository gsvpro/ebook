# Chapter 53: Coherence Breaks

Luc Moreau's funeral had no body.

The kinetic strike that destroyed the shelter node left debris, pressure scars, and a manual hatch sealed from the outside. It did not leave enough of Luc for the rituals his family preferred. Mother Aveline performed the service anyway, because repair before worship did not mean worship never mattered. It meant grief also deserved maintenance.

Fabron stood in uniform beside Luc's parents and looked carved from rules that no longer fit.

Lars attended at the back.

That was not humility. It was cowardice with manners, and Mother Aveline saw through it before the first hymn ended.

"Forward," she said.

He went.

Luc had known only fragments: shelter routes, refusal doctrine, enough about Black Hole to be afraid, not enough about Aerits to become doctrinally dangerous. He had died saving families who would never know why the hatch held. The network recorded his action as sacrifice, not proof that sacrifice should be demanded again.

After the service, Fabron found Lars in the tool chapel.

"I want names," Fabron said.

"Black Hole cell?"

"Yes."

"We have six."

"Give them to me."

There was no humor in him. No stiff irritation. Just grief seeking a command shape.

Lars knew the feeling too well to trust either of them with it.

"No."

Fabron's eyes went flat. "He was my brother in the order."

"Yes."

"He died because he followed a protocol you brought into our lives."

"Yes."

The word cost more than defense would have.

Fabron stepped close. "Then do not deny me repair."

"Revenge is not repair."

"Do not quote my own house at me."

"Then don't make it necessary."

For a moment Lars thought Fabron would hit him. Part of him welcomed it. Another part, older and uglier, calculated twelve ways to stop him without injury and hated itself.

Mira entered quietly. She had come for the funeral against her own habits. "Major, I have the cell evidence. I will not release it for extrajudicial action. I will release it to three jurisdictions, your order's review council, and the victim-family advocate."

Fabron turned on her. "You are not in my chain."

"Correct. That is why I am useful."

The coherence broke there, not with shouting but with withdrawal. Fabron left. For two days he answered only operational necessities. His order continued shelter work. The network held. The friendship, if that was the word for mutual irritation under fire, did not.

On the third day, the evidence chain produced arrests in two systems and one shootout Lars did not attend. Luc's parents received a full account of what could be told and a sealed debt ledger for what could not.

Fabron returned to Fastulv with red eyes and an immaculate uniform.

"You were right," he said.

Lars shook his head. "Not cleanly."

"No. But sufficiently."

They stood in bad silence.

Then Fabron added, "I still hate you."

"Coherence, then."

Fabron's mouth trembled once.

"Coherence."
