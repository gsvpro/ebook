# Chapter 22: After the Choice

The facility survived by becoming less elegant.

Aphex emergency architecture had preferred central command, clean dashboards, and reassuring chains of authorization. The people of L-Prime 41 survived because machinists jammed hatch gears with pry bars, garden crews moved oxygen canisters by hand, reactor techs ignored a remote lockout, and a night-cook named Serafina knew where the old pressure curtains were because she had used them to store mushrooms.

By local dawn, the brown dwarf was still not a sun, but the station's windows glowed red with it anyway.

Mira refused sedation. She allowed a sling, two pain-block patches, and one cup of coffee so violent Grim asked for a sample.

"No," she told him.

"For science."

"Especially no."

They met in the materials lab because Mira wanted honest machinery nearby. The cracked beam still sat on the table. Around it, Aphex guards pretended not to listen while doing exactly that.

Lars placed three evidence packets on Mira's console. "Hostile tissue analysis. Attack telemetry. Your own sensor derivation. No memory work."

"And the fourth packet?"

He did not touch it. "Unlock proposal. Partial. Reversible boundaries where possible. Consent ledger stored outside Aphex and outside me."

Mira stared at the sealed file for a long time.

"Will I remember being someone else?"

"Maybe."

"Will that make me less myself?"

Lars almost answered quickly. Valdrun's quiet voice in his hidden channel said nothing, which was somehow sterner than advice.

"It made me more responsible for myself," Lars said. "Not happier. Not cleaner. More responsible."

Mira absorbed that like a load test.

Fabron stood by the door with his hands behind his back. He had not slept. His uniform still looked offensive. "Doctor, Aphex can protect you here while you decide."

"Aphex was penetrated here."

"Yes. Which means we know where some holes are."

"That is almost humility, Major. Careful."

"I will recover."

Mira smiled faintly, then looked at Lars. "No unlock today."

Lars felt disappointment, relief, and shame that disappointment had arrived first.

"Understood."

"I want protection protocols. I want my lab hardened. I want a copy of all evidence related to the attack. I want to know when people look for me. And I want the right to ask later without you hovering like a tragic weapon rack."

Grim said, "That can be documented."

"Also, you do not access my medical content without my written authorization. Metadata was already too much."

Lars nodded. "Agreed."

"Say it properly."

He looked at her then, not as a candidate, not as a lost Aerit, not as a clue Lyra might have left behind.

As Mira.

"Your life is yours. We protect without possession. We answer questions you ask. We stop when you say stop."

The sealed fourth packet deleted itself from her console with a soft tone.

Mira exhaled once. "Good."

Then she surprised him.

"That word."

Lars waited.

"Coherence," she said, careful and sharp, as if testing a material sample.

The lab air seemed to pause. No old migraine. No lock surge. Only a resonance in Lars's chest, like hearing a door stay closed because the person inside had chosen it.

"Coherence?" she asked.

Valdrun's hidden breath caught.

Lars answered softly. "Coherence."

Mira did not awaken. She did not become ancient. She did not collapse into someone else's century. But her posture changed by a degree, and her eyes went wet with anger rather than confusion.

"I hate that I missed it," she said.

"Missed what?"

"I don't know. That's why I hate it."

The cost came three hours later.

Aphex headquarters froze Mira's promotion track pending medical review. Someone inside the corporation had smelled value and liability. Marchand protested. Fabron escalated. Arsenault sent a message that contained sympathy, legal cover, and three hidden clauses Lars marked for later violence.

Mira read the suspension notice twice.

"There it is," she said. "The institution protecting me from the consequences of being attacked."

Lars expected rage. Instead she opened a new work order.

"What are you doing?"

"Auditing every manual bulkhead in this facility. If they want me idle, I will become structurally inconvenient."

Fabron's expression shifted into something like respect.

On the hidden channel, Havets Sista Sång sent a quiet packet: residue from one breached corridor contained unstable nanomaterial not matching Black Hole stock. It had appeared, done nothing hostile, and decayed while watching the fight.

Lyra's apparatus, perhaps.

A witness. A guardian. Or an auditor older than Aphex.

Lars looked at the evidence, then at Mira, who had chosen delay and still chosen work.

For the first time since Arkology Zero, he understood that rescue could be real even when nobody came home.
