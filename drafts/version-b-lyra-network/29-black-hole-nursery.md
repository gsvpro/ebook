# Chapter 29: Black Hole Nursery

The red nursery was not in a black hole.

Lars found that disappointing in the petty part of himself that appreciated honest branding. The facility orbited a captured rogue planet wrapped in heat sinks and false mining claims. It hid by looking poor. Black Hole B-7 had learned that wealth attracted audits and poverty attracted only condolences.

Ruun's reflected image had given them enough geometry to build a probability cone. Vigor's cattle route gave them residue. Mira's fatigue analysis identified hull-tendon composites matching the L-Prime attackers. Grim stitched it together with procurement fraud from Aphex, missing NUKEA bioreactor membranes, and a Syren sermon about blood as invitation.

"We observe," Valdrun said.

"We always observe right before doing something inadvisable," Lars said.

Havets held them outside normal sensor reach. Fastulv drifted three light-minutes away as a derelict hauler broadcasting complaints about unpaid docking fees.

The nursery unfolded on their displays in layers. Outer ring: stolen cutter frames. Middle ring: tissue growth vats around ceramic skeletons. Inner ring: blood processing and particulate training chambers. Black Hole was not merely stealing biological samples. It was trying to grow soldiers that could tolerate Aerit-derived nanotech long enough to weaponize fragments of it.

It was failing.

The failures were the horror.

Tanks held bodies that had never been people and bodies that might have been. Some were clones grown around stolen genetic scaffolds. Some were kidnapped organisms from four species. Neural activity ranged from absent to screaming.

Mira, present by encrypted relay, went silent.

Fabron crossed himself with two fingers and then looked angry at his own reflex.

"Evidence package," Lars said, voice flat. "Full chain. No public dump until victims are protected."

Grim's lenses dimmed. "Already recording."

They found Ruun in the inner ring.

Not whole. Not captured in the ordinary sense. A guardian's mind-signature flickered through a maintenance swarm, spread across diagnostic dust, coolant valves, and the timing jitter of red nursery lights. Ruun had not escaped. Ruun had become sabotage.

A message emerged from the noise.

Do not pull. Too many connected. Break appetite, not wall.

Lars closed his hand until his knuckles hurt.

"They are using victims as hostages against rescue."

"Yes," Valdrun said. "So we do not rescue like fools."

The strike was not a strike. Havets altered thermal rejection timing. NUKEA recall kits already seeded in nearby depots failed in a way that looked like supplier scandal and forced emergency shutdown of the nursery's membrane stock. Aphex dissenters froze three procurement accounts. Vigor's traders bought every compatible nutrient feed within two jumps and redirected it to famine relief with excellent paperwork.

Inside the nursery, growth slowed.

Not stopped. Slowed.

Enough for Ruun's distributed sabotage to open six victim tanks into emergency stasis rather than disposal.

Enough for Grim to copy identities.

Enough for Lars to mark every ship that fled.

A Black Hole commander appeared on an intercepted channel, face hidden behind ritual void paint. "You cannot save what you will not seize."

Lars almost answered.

Then he did not give the predator a center to bite.

Fabron sent the reply through a minor compliance office: Due to irregularities in biological membrane certification, your operation is subject to quarantine pending review.

It was absurd.

It worked for nine hours.

In those nine hours, sixty-three victims entered stasis, seventeen shell accounts burned, and the red nursery lost the ability to grow harvest craft at scale.

Black Hole B-7 remained dangerous.

But its appetite had met logistics.
