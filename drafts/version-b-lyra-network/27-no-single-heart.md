# Chapter 27: No Single Heart

The hub was called Mercy Exchange, which meant it had once been built by optimists and later purchased by people with invoices.

It linked food, medical gels, reactor spares, and pressure fabrics across twelve marginal colonies. Vigor's table used it. NUKEA's false recall leaned on it. Fabron's order had three quiet warehouses in its lower ring. Grim's model rated it dangerously useful.

Black Hole found the same rating.

The first attack was not a missile. It was a compliance audit.

Licenses failed. Insurance bonds expired. Customs seals mismatched by one digit. Reactor shielding crates were held for tariff review. Food protein spoiled in legal limbo while a thousand small habitats waited for shipments that had no heroic story attached to them.

"They learned," Grim said. "They are attacking logistics instead of bodies."

"Bodies die when logistics fail," Vigor said, already moving.

Every instinct in Lars wanted to save Mercy Exchange. It was a hub. Hubs were visible. Hubs were satisfying. A man could fly in, expose the corruption, humiliate officials, maybe break a few doors, and feel useful in a way that made songs stupidly easy.

Lyra's doctrine, grown over fifty years after Pluto and sharpened over centuries, had a different answer.

No single heart.

They let Mercy Exchange fail safely.

Not collapse. Not burn. Fail safely.

For three days, Vigor's network rerouted shipments through farm convoys, funeral transports, religious pilgrim lockers, Aphex warranty returns, and a children's robotics tournament that unexpectedly required seven tons of coolant membrane. NUKEA technicians taught colony crews to fabricate substitute seals. Station guilds opened old caches. Fabron's order triggered a ritual fast that, in practice, reduced food demand and freed cargo mass for medicine.

Lars stayed off every public channel.

It felt like cowardice until he watched Black Hole's purchased auditors arrive at Mercy Exchange and find a hub stripped of decisive value. The warehouses were empty. The records were honest enough to be useless. The people had moved authority outward.

Arsenault called him privately. "You sacrificed a market position."

"I saved the colonies."

"Both are true."

"Cry into a dividend."

"Already scheduled."

Mercy Exchange entered receivership under three shell entities, none controlled by Lars. One was an Abantu cooperative. One was a NUKEA retiree pension fund. One was a monastery Fabron swore was not his, although the receiver's seal contained a cracked line.

The system did not become stronger because a hero defended the heart.

It became stronger because the heart stopped being singular.

That night, Lars sat in Fastulv's galley while Grim attempted bread and produced a structurally interesting brick.

"You are grieving a warehouse," Grim said.

"I am grieving the simplicity of punching the right person."

"There are still persons to punch."

"You always know what to say."

Grim placed the brick on a plate. "I also made food."

"That is a legal claim."

A message arrived from Mira: Good call. Hated it. Evidence attached.

The evidence showed Black Hole watchers confused by supply flows that looked organic because they were organic. Not perfect. Not clean. Human, alien, stubborn, locally stupid, locally brilliant.

Lars looked at the model and removed himself from the center again.

This time Grim did not restore him immediately.
