# Chapter 26: The Second Guardian Channel

The message arrived folded inside a livestock quarantine report.

Vigor found it because she read every document that tried to tell her where animals could not go. The report concerned horn-moss cattle, fungal hoof rot, and a transport route through three minor stations. Buried in the checksum drift was an old guardian authentication pattern, degraded but real.

Not Valdrun.

Not Ödesvaka.

Not Lars.

Grim isolated the packet inside a Faraday room, a quantum-noise bath, and a stew pot Vigor insisted had sentimental shielding properties.

"The pot is irrelevant," Grim said.

"Then it will not hurt."

The decoded signal contained eleven seconds of audio, two corrupted star maps, and a phrase spoken in an Aerit dialect Lars remembered only after hearing it. The remembering did not hurt like the old locks. It opened with a dull ache of grief, like finding a tool worn to the shape of a dead friend's hand.

Coherence uncertain. Channel salted. Do not gather. Watch the red nursery. Lyra's little hands are in the walls.

Then static.

Fabron, who had insisted on being present because the universe kept involving his childhood religion without paperwork, said, "Little hands?"

Valdrun answered through the hidden channel, voice dry as old wire. "Manifestation agents, perhaps. Or drones. Or a warning from someone concussed."

"Which guardian?" Lars asked.

Ödesvaka supplied the probability tree from Arkology Zero. The sender signature matched Ruun-of-Intervals, one of the seven who had vanished into deep infrastructure after the hypnosis project. Ruun had specialized in relay cultures, patient sabotage, and making empires trip over their own filing habits.

"That sounds useful," Fabron said.

"Ruun once delayed a hostile fleet by convincing it to standardize bolt sizes," Lars said.

"I withdraw the understatement."

The compromised part mattered more. Channel salted meant someone had used the guardian path as bait. The red nursery matched Grim's growing model of Black Hole biological acquisition: not one lab, but a distributed growth infrastructure using stolen bodies, cloned frames, and nanobot hunger it did not understand.

Mira joined by secure delay. "If the signal is bait, we should not answer."

"If we don't answer, Ruun may die," Lars said.

"Or may already be gone. Or may be waiting to see whether you centralize response."

He disliked that because she sounded like Valdrun.

They built three replies and sent none of them.

Instead, Vigor routed cattle shipments through the star map's least important coordinate. NUKEA scheduled a regulator inspection at the second. Fabron's order sent two human brothers to repair a chapel door at the third, carrying no weapons, only old songs and ceramic sensor dust in their boot soles.

Havets watched the paths from outside ordinary approach geometry.

Forty-six hours later, the cattle convoy reported fungal hoof rot was under control. Hidden in the vet's image packet was a reflection in a stainless trough: a corridor no livestock ship possessed, a red-lit tank, and a hand pressed against glass from inside.

Ruun, perhaps.

Or bait wearing hope.

Lars wanted to launch.

Valdrun said, "No single heart."

He closed his eyes, breathed through the ache, and did not become the center.

"Send shelters," he said. "Send watchers. No rescue fleet. Not yet."

Grim looked at him with metal patience.

"Coherence?"

Lars answered like it cost him, because it did.

"Coherence."
