# Chapter 38: Lyra's Bad Body

The holojournal was hidden in Havets Sista Sång under a maintenance warning labeled DO NOT OPEN UNLESS LARS IS BEING PARTICULARLY STUPID. Valdrun denied writing it. Ödesvaka denied nothing. Lyra appeared in the recording inside a temporary body that was failing with spectacular indignity. One eye focused three seconds late. Her left hand kept trying to disassemble a cup. Her hair floated as if gravity had become a suggestion only it rejected. 'If this works,' she told the recorder, 'I am a genius. If it does not, delete the part where I walked into the cabinet.' The date placed it decades after Pluto, when Återviens had begun forming from survival practice but had not yet hardened into doctrine. She described preserved-body projection as engineering, not miracle: a stable biological anchor, nanotech-mediated field coupling, temporary matter scaffold, ruthless mass accounting, and a humiliating number of failed knees. The body collapsed at minute nine, leaving residue with the same instability Havets had found near later unexplained events. Lars laughed once, then stopped because grief had entered quietly. Lyra, looking half-drunk from neural lag, leaned toward the recorder. 'Do not let them make me a prophet. If I am absent, ask better questions. If I appear, demand evidence.'
