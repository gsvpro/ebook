# Chapter 33: Coherence Break

The argument began over a rescue window and became a referendum on civilization. Black Hole had three suspected hidden Aerits moving through separate systems. One was confirmed by blood-market chatter, one by Syren rumor, one only by a pattern in hospital procurement data. Lars wanted all three shielded before Black Hole could triangulate. Valdrun wanted only the confirmed case touched; every contact created signal. Grim produced models until the room looked like an autopsy of possible futures. Mira watched the models and said the thing no one wanted said: 'You are using consent as if danger politely waits outside it.' Lars snapped back too fast. Fabron stepped between them, not because he understood Aerit trauma, but because soldiers understood rooms before they became battlefields. 'Then build protection that does not require identity,' he said. Vigor backed him. Local redundancy, not heroic extraction. For the first time since the partial unlock, Lars felt an old emotional overload rise hot in his chest. No migraine. No lock. Just memory: too many people lost because he had arrived late. He left the room before anger became command. Mira found him in Fastulv's cargo bay. She did not apologize. Neither did he. Instead they rewrote the plan: one direct ask, two blind shields, no forced awakenings, and public reasons boring enough to live inside.
