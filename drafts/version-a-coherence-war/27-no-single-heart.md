# Chapter 27: No Single Heart

The central hub wanted saving because central hubs always believed they were civilization. Relay Nine controlled insurance routing, medical authorization mirrors, convoy traffic predictions, and enough Aphex emergency bandwidth to make Arsenault sweat through expensive fabric. Black Hole seeded it with a logic worm that would either expose hidden medical anomalies or crash half the sector's supply approvals. The clean solution was to seize the hub and purge it. The Återviens solution was uglier: let the hub fail safely while local systems learned to stand. Lars ordered staged degradation. Arsenault called him insane. Fabron called him partially insane but tactically coherent. Mira built a failure map showing which colonies would lose medicine unless substitute channels opened within six hours. Vigor's people opened them in four. NUKEA technicians ran paper manifests. Farm barges carried insulin. Aphex captains accepted local traffic authority. Havets hid the worst traffic gaps by bending sensor confidence just enough to prevent panic. When Relay Nine finally went dark, the sector did not die. It stumbled, cursed, improvised, and kept breathing. Black Hole lost its data harvest. Aphex lost the illusion that one hub could be trusted with everything. Lars watched the backup channels bloom across Grim's display like fungus, or stars. 'No single heart,' Valdrun said. Lars answered, 'Coherence.'
