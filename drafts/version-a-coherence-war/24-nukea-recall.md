# Chapter 24: NUKEA Recall

The NUKEA recall looked boring enough to save lives. A batch of modular reactor governors had developed timing drift under long radiation exposure, which meant every station using them had to accept inspection teams or risk insurance collapse. Lars loved it immediately. Under the recall cover, Vigor's farms shipped replacement ceramics, Aphex sent escort craft, and Grim embedded diagnostic packages that detected Black Hole blood-market sniffers hiding in power telemetry. The vulnerable colony was Mbeki's Rest, a water-poor settlement built inside an old comet rind. Its reactor hall smelled of ammonia, hot shielding, and people pretending not to be afraid. Mira inspected fatigue scars in the governor frame and found deliberate wear, a sabotage pattern spread over eleven years. Fabron organized the evacuation lanes and argued with three local officials until they obeyed because his patience was more frightening than shouting. When Black Hole drones arrived disguised as recall inspectors, Fastulv intercepted in full view while Havets quietly folded a heat sink into the drones' blind geometry and cooked their guidance cores from the inside. No one saw the old ship. Everyone saw local technicians refusing remote shutdown orders and keeping the reactor stable by hand. Afterward, the colony thanked NUKEA. Lars let them. The truth would have made them targets. Mira kept one cracked timing crystal and wrote in her own file: systems fail where trust has already been stolen.
