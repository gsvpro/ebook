# Chapter 25: Vigor's Table

Vigor fought wars with soup. Lars had mocked this until he watched her feed Aphex soldiers, comet miners, smugglers, reactor techs, and three frightened children in the same pressurized dining hall without letting any of them realize they had become a logistics network. The table sat beneath grow lights on a farm barge that smelled of basil, yeast, and engine oil. Vigor moved between benches with a ladle and a tactical map hidden in her apron display. She knew which crews could hide a person, which stations had honest doctors, which smugglers were cowards but not traitors, and which Aphex quartermasters would route spare shielding if the request mentioned children instead of strategy. Fabron distrusted her for eight minutes. Then she corrected his casualty projection by naming two maintenance guilds his database lacked. Mira sat quietly, eating like someone who had forgotten hunger could be simple. When a Syren recruiter tried to turn the attack at L-Prime into a sermon about chosen blood, Vigor handed him a bowl, listened, and then asked who repaired his air filters. He had no answer. The room heard the failure. Lars realized the war would not be won only by ships. It would be won by kitchens, manifests, spare valves, and people who knew when myth was trying to steal labor's clothes.
