# Chapter 55: A Quiet Signal

Afterward, nobody got the ending they wanted. Arsenault got a survivable Aphex and fewer levers. Fabron got command citations, old Order messages, and the terrible knowledge that his childhood rituals had been load-bearing history. Vigor got more mouths to feed. Grim got damaged hands, new recipes, and a private archive of Sera's last channel he would not admit he replayed. Mira got her life, altered but still hers. She did not unlock fully. She asked for one controlled memory fragment weeks later, with Lars, Grim, Fabron, and her own stop command present. What returned was not a revelation of destiny but a corridor, bread cooling on a rail, someone asking 'Coherence?' and Mira's own old voice answering, 'Coherence.' She cried afterward from overload and anger and relief. Lars did not touch the moment. He made coffee badly and let her insult it.

Black Hole B-7 survived as cells, predators without their central harvest. Syren survived as rumor. The Creator doors were mapped but not mastered. The Nomad hypothesis remained a hypothesis with teeth. The galaxy heard a public story about cartel defeat, reactor reforms, and heroic local coordination. Under it, Återviens spread faster because people had practiced it and lived.

On Arkology Zero, Havets Sista Sång delivered one final packet from the fold shelters Lyra had seeded. No body appeared. No voice. Only sensor data from the KBC Void: a quiet region becoming fractionally less quiet, as if something vast had turned its attention without approaching.

The packet contained a greeting in old format and no sender authentication anyone could trust.

Coherence?

Lars stood with Valdrun, Grim, and the records of the seven reduced by one. He felt no migraine, no command from the past, no permission to become a king. Only the long work ahead, measured in decades and centuries, and the stubborn relief that the first book of disaster had not ended in a throne.

He opened a blank response file.

After a while, he typed one word.

Coherence.
