# Chapter 54: Re-Becoming

The final battle looked, from outside, like a series of unrelated malfunctions. Black Hole ships missed burns. Their grown hulls split along fatigue paths Mira had predicted and local miners had seeded with dust. Their command relays drowned in health-code traffic, reactor logs, soup invoices, and false blood signatures. Aphex attempted to seize central coordination twice; Arsenault stopped the first attempt for strategy and Fabron stopped the second with soldiers in hallways. Fastulv took punishment where cameras could see. Havets Sista Sång did the real killing quietly, cutting fold options, preserving escape lanes for prisoners, and forcing Black Hole's flagship to choose between mass acquisition and reactor stability. It chose acquisition. That was why it lost. Lars boarded only at the end, not as savior but as auditor with a gun, Grim at his side and Fabron behind him swearing in French. They found the acquisition core surrounded by stolen samples and failed bodies. Lars did not make a speech. He uploaded the evidence, opened the prisoner locks, and let Mira's fatigue model guide one Ragnarrök shot into the core's structural lie. The flagship did not explode. It came apart in sections, each failure contained by systems Black Hole had considered inefficient. Distributed safety beat centralized hunger. By dawn, B-7 was broken into fugitive cells. Dangerous still. Smaller. Bleeding.
