# Chapter 35: The Creator Map

The Creator map was hidden inside the nursery's waste archive because Black Hole had mistaken it for reactor geometry. Grim reconstructed it from compression scars, old isotopic labels, and a checksum written in mathematics older than Aphex by half a million years. It showed portal distribution across dead systems, brown dwarf chains, and quiet gravitational shelves. It did not align with trade, colonization, or military sense. It aligned with artifacts like Pluto. Mira hated the first model and built three alternatives. All failed. The portals clustered around places where older nanostructure had already changed vacuum noise. 'The Creators did not invent the road,' she said. 'They paved over tracks.' Lars remembered Lyra on Pluto, frost on her eyelashes, laughing because fear had become less useful than measurement. Fabron asked the soldier's question: could Black Hole use the map? Grim answered yes, badly, which was worse than no. Havets Sista Sång held the recovered fragments in a fold-isolated vault while Ödesvaka ran age models. The result made the room quiet. The Creators were not gods, not ancestors, not the first makers. They were brilliant engineers who had copied a communication layer and turned it into transport without understanding what had been communicating.
