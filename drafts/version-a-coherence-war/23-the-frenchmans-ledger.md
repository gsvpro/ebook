# Chapter 23: The Frenchman's Ledger

Gabriel Arsenault kept two ledgers. One held contracts, weapons platforms, insurance shells, and the ordinary sins of a corporation old enough to have legal departments with family trees. The other held nightmares. Lars saw both when Arsenault opened a private channel from a ship that officially was not moving toward L-Prime 41. The chairman admitted nothing cleanly. Profit mattered. French influence mattered. Aphex survival mattered. But he also showed collapsed convoy routes, uninsurable colonies, reactor dependencies, and Black Hole raids that followed medical anomalies rather than cargo value. 'If your hidden people are real,' Arsenault said, 'they are already in my risk model.' Lars hated him less for saying it plainly. Mira, listening by choice, asked why the model had not protected the vulnerable. Arsenault answered, 'Because models report incentives. They do not create courage.' Fabron called that convenient. Grim called it statistically correct and morally insufficient. The ledger became their first war map: Aphex assets that could be used without becoming a throne, colonies that needed hardening, salvage brokers feeding Black Hole, and one NUKEA recall notice placed so perfectly that Lars smelled bait. By the end, Arsenault offered ships, lawyers, and deniable money. Lars accepted only distributed access, local authority, and evidence custody split five ways. Arsenault sighed like a man watching profit become ethics. 'You make ownership difficult.' Lars said, 'That is the point.'
