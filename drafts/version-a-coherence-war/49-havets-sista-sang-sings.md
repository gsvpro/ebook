# Chapter 49: Havets Sista Sång Sings

Havets Sista Sång sang without sound across six systems. The song was timing, gravity, false certainty, and old hull intelligence moving where no public sensor could swear it had been. It made Black Hole couriers miss rendezvous by seconds. It made Aphex central servers appear less reliable than local caches. It hid three evacuation barges inside comet glare. It carried a temporary-body residue sample to a guardian archive and returned with a list of fold-safe shelters Lyra had seeded two centuries earlier. Lars stood on Fastulv's bridge and watched only the consequences: routes opening, raids arriving late, frightened people finding doors unlocked by someone who knew maintenance codes from before their grandparents were born. Grim translated fragments into music for his own amusement and immediately regretted it because the result made Vigor cry. Mira did not cry. She asked whether the ship was making decisions or executing old doctrine. Valdrun answered, 'Yes.' The largest Black Hole task group tried to triangulate the song and found Fastulv instead, broadcasting obscene Aphex audit notices. Lars grinned as missiles lit the display. Visible wolf. Hidden sea. The old coordination finally worked at fleet scale.
