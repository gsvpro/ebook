# Chapter 28: Fabron Sees Too Much

Fabron confronted Lars in a cargo lock because it was the only place aboard Fastulv with honest acoustics and no chairs to weaponize. He had collected inconsistencies: Lars knowing obsolete Aphex hull failures before declassification, Grim behaving like a sovereign intelligence, Mira's biology, Vigor's impossible logistics, the old Order message naming the black wolf, and sensor ghosts where Havets Sista Sång should have been. 'You are not merely a lucky criminal with a board seat,' Fabron said. Lars considered lying. Instead he showed Fabron one piece of evidence: a Pluto image, Lyra's gloved hand beside an artifact whose nanostructure matched residue from the Black Hole probe. No speeches. No origin myth. Just chain of custody, spectral layers, and dates that made Fabron's face go rigid. The major touched the steel token under his uniform. 'My grandmother prayed over broken pumps,' he said. 'You are telling me she inherited maintenance doctrine from aliens.' Lars said, 'I am telling you she knew repair mattered.' Fabron hated that answer because it preserved both dignity and evidence. He did not ask to join. He asked what non-Aerit humans could do without becoming followers of a secret throne. Lars smiled sadly. 'Exactly the right question.'
