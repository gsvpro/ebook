# Chapter 21: Containment Drill

Lars made himself the loudest target by walking into the main spine of Dependency L-Prime 41 and demanding a chair.

Not a command chair. Not a security console. A chair from the executive chapel module, because nothing told a hostile boarding force where to aim quite like a board director dragging expensive furniture into the middle of an evacuation corridor.

Fabron followed him with six soldiers, one medical technician, and the expression of a man composing a resignation letter in French. "This is not a defensible position."

"It is emotionally defensible."

"That is not a category in tactical doctrine."

"You have dull doctrine."

The facility shuddered as the three approaching ships cut through the brown dwarf's particle wake. Mira's voice came over the local tactical net, steady and cold. "Their hulls are not stolen. They are grown around stolen frames. Fatigue compensation is organic, but the thrust cage is Aphex pattern, four years old. Someone bought wreckage and taught it to bleed."

Grim added, "I dislike that sentence for both technical and aesthetic reasons."

"Can they breach?" Fabron asked.

"They do not need to," Mira said. "They are loading kinetic needles into the wake. The wake will do the steering."

Lars looked through the pressure glass at the brown dwarf's dim fire. The old him, before the partial unlock, would have felt the lock trying to punish recognition. Now there was no migraine, no blade behind the eyes. There was only memory resonance, grief folded with geometry: Lyra laughing over Pluto dust, Valdrun swearing at broken pumps, a young engineering team learning that every system wanted to become a throne unless someone kept cutting the legs.

"Fastulv," Lars said, "be loud."

The freighter undocked on public telemetry with all the subtlety of a drunk whale. Its transponder screamed corporate priority. Its engines flared too hot. Its defensive grid advertised failures Grim had invented with great artistic care.

The three Black Hole craft turned toward it.

"Havets," Lars said through the hidden channel, "be impolite."

Havets Sista Sång did not appear. It did not fire in any spectrum Aphex could name. It moved inside a shallow fold, touching ordinary space at four points for less than a second each. At each touch, it released seed drones the size of fingernails into the brown dwarf wake.

Mira saw the effect first. "The particle stream is changing. You are adding drag nodes."

"We are adding lies," Grim said. "The kind physics believes briefly."

The first kinetic needle curved early and struck a maintenance array instead of the habitation cylinder. The second tumbled into the brown dwarf's radiation belt. The third still came on target.

Fabron did not wait for Lars. "Team Three, shutter line. Manual lock. Ignore remote overrides. Move."

His soldiers moved. No speech, no drama. They sprinted into a corridor where the automatic doors had already begun cycling under hostile command. Sergeant Mbeki jammed a foam charge into the hinge and fired. Ceramic armor bloomed like white coral, freezing the door half-closed.

The third needle hit.

The pressure wave did not breach the station, but it cracked the materials ring's outer sensor boom and threw half the lab into emergency darkness. Lars felt the structure complain under his boots. Mira, somewhere below, said one word that was probably not in any Aphex safety manual.

Then the captured hostile in the med bay woke up properly.

It did not break the coffin. It convinced the coffin it had always been open.

Grim cursed in three machine languages. Lars was already running.

Fabron caught up because irritating men stayed alive by being fit. "The med bay is behind two sealed bulkheads."

"Then it is trapped."

"Or we are."

"Both can be true."

They reached the med bay as black particulate crawled out through the ventilation seam. It moved like smoke with intent, searching for blood heat. Fabron fired foam. Lars fired the Ragnarrök at minimum yield again, shaving the air above the floor and turning a meter of particulate into inert dust. The rest recoiled.

Mira appeared from the side corridor with a sensor pack slung over her injured shoulder. "It is using thermal gradients. Stop heating it."

"My gun is heat," Lars said.

"Then be less yourself."

Fabron made a strangled approving noise.

Mira slapped the sensor pack against the wall and overrode the local climate system. The corridor temperature dropped twenty degrees in twelve seconds. Condensation flashed into ice across the floor. The particulate slowed, confused by a world that no longer smelled alive.

Grim arrived through the ceiling, because apparently he had decided doors were for people with limited imagination. He dropped onto the coffin, drove both hands through its control core, and said, "Consent ledger intact. Hostile interrogation no longer feasible."

The thing inside the coffin split itself rather than be taken. Black strands snapped inward. Tissue collapsed. A pulse of corrupt nanomaterial struck Grim's chassis and smoked.

Lars grabbed him before he fell.

"Damage?"

"Mostly pride. Some hand."

Mira stared at the dead hostile, then at the black stain on Grim's metal palm. "That was not an assassin."

"No," Lars said.

Fabron looked at him. "A probe."

The station alarms shifted pitch. External telemetry showed the three Black Hole craft withdrawing, not defeated, not routed, but satisfied with what they had learned.

Mira leaned against the wall, breathing hard. "They came to measure the response."

Lars nodded. "Containment drill."

"Whose?" Fabron asked.

Through the hidden channel, Valdrun answered, old and grim. "Theirs. And now ours."
