# Chapter 46: Wake No More Than Needed

The hardest meeting had no guns in it. Seven guardian channels, not all visual, aligned through Havets Sista Sång for twenty-three minutes under fold isolation. Valdrun spoke for caution. Ödesvaka spoke for protocol. A guardian called Sera of the Long Rooms wanted broader activation before Black Hole killed people who might have defended themselves. Another, whose voice arrived as pressure modulation through an ice archive, argued that waking hidden Aerits under panic would make them targets and tools. Lars listened until the old impatience clawed at him. Then Mira, who had no vote and every right to leave, asked what data distinguished necessary waking from fear. That saved them from doctrine theater. They built a limited activation protocol: shield first, ask only under immediate identity-specific threat, partial evidence before memory, no full unlock without sustained consent, and human/local allies treated as primary defenses rather than scenery. Jun Vale remained locked. Mira remained partial. Two others accepted facts but not names. One accepted emergency motor-memory release to survive an attack and wept afterward because consent did not erase cost. Lars recorded every boundary. No one was rescued into ownership. No one was left unwatched because purity felt cleaner than responsibility.
