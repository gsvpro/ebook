# Chapter 42: The Nomad Hypothesis

Lyra's later model arrived in pieces from three places no one had connected: a corrupted Havets archive, residue from the bad body journal, and a temporary-body trace found inside a locked Aphex evidence vault with no access log. The model was cautious, full of crossed-out certainty. Artifacts were routers. Nanobots were local hosts and translators. Biological minds could couple to a wider field if they carried compatible structure and consent-stable identity boundaries. The Nomads were not ghosts. They were civilizations, or descendants of civilizations, that had learned to preserve pattern across low-interaction regions without becoming visible empires. Mira demanded experiments. Grim designed only safe ones. Lars vetoed three because Lyra's handwriting in the margins said, in effect, do not be a heroic idiot here. Fabron asked the practical question: what did the hypothesis change in tomorrow's battle? It changed targeting. Black Hole could not be allowed to centralize stolen nanotech because central coercion broke the very coherence it wanted, producing monsters, probes, and failed bodies. Syren could not be allowed to turn coupling into surrender. The answer remained annoyingly old: local choice, hard evidence, distributed shields, and no throne.
