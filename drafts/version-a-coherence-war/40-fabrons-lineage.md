# Chapter 40: Fabron's Lineage

Fabron brought them to a chapel that was not a chapel if one knew how to read tools. It sat behind a reactor school on a moon that paid taxes to three governments and loyalty to none. The walls held no saints, only diagrams of pumps, emergency gardens, pressure doors, broken circles, and hands repairing things together. The elder who met them wore a mechanic's apron over formal black and greeted Fabron by his childhood name, which delighted Lars until Fabron threatened him with paperwork. The Order of the Clear Hand did not know Aerits. It knew rules: refuse the throne, keep the hinge small, feed before preaching, repair before worship, never let one archive become truth. Its stories mentioned clear ones, old wolves, sleeping engineers, and a woman of star-dust who hated kneeling. Over centuries, the order had forgotten origins and preserved function. Lars told them less than they wanted and more than he liked. Mira listened as an outsider who might be older than the building. Fabron stood between worlds, furious and moved. He was not secretly Aerit. He was worse, Lars thought with affection: proof the idea had escaped biology. When Black Hole tried to trace the meeting, it found twenty-seven similar orders sending contradictory maintenance bulletins across the sector. The human bridge had opened, and it did not belong to Lars.
