# Chapter 26: The Second Guardian Channel

The signal arrived folded inside agricultural pricing data from an Abantu grain exchange. Grim found it because the decimal errors tasted old. Valdrun recognized the cadence. Ödesvaka confirmed the authority with a reluctance that made Lars nervous. A second guardian was awake, or something with a guardian's keys wanted them to believe so. The message contained three coordinates, one warning, and the greeting Lars had not expected to feel in his chest: Coherence? Mira watched him read it and asked whether that word always hurt. 'Not like it used to,' Lars said. 'Now it reminds me I have debts.' The coordinates pointed to a dead relay, a NUKEA archive, and a refugee hospital that had treated five impossible survivals in thirty years. The warning said Black Hole had learned to follow consent events. Not awakenings, not blood scans: consent events. Someone was watching when hidden people were asked. That changed everything. Fabron proposed compartmentalization. Grim proposed false asks using synthetic ledgers. Vigor proposed asking fewer questions and delivering more tools. Lars chose all three. They sent no answer on the old channel. Instead, Havets Sista Sång released a passive listening web across fold-adjacent routes. If a guardian was calling, they would protect them. If a predator was wearing an old voice, they would count its teeth.
