# Chapter 32: The Syren Invitation

The Syren Collective invited Lars to negotiate in a theater carved into an abandoned luxury liner. That alone should have been enough to shoot someone. They promised safe passage, public truth, protection for hidden Aerits, and an audience large enough to turn fear into doctrine. Lars went because traps were evidence with furniture. Fabron went because someone had to disapprove professionally. Mira went by choice, wearing a fatigue sensor mesh under her jacket. The Syren speaker was young, beautiful, and empty in the way zealots became when they surrendered uncertainty. She called Aerits the returning choir and Black Hole the necessary darkness that would reveal them. Lars let her talk until her metaphors became operational: she had lists, donors, leaked Aphex medical flags, and a plan to stage a failed rescue as martyrdom. Grim captured every packet. Havets mapped every hidden transmitter. When Syren sprung the trap, expecting Lars to denounce them and confirm the myth, he instead complained about their accounting. Fabron arrested three financiers on Aphex fraud warrants. Mira exposed the theater's structural rot over public maintenance channels, forcing evacuation for mundane safety reasons. The spectacle collapsed into paperwork. Lars left without correcting the lie, because sometimes starving a myth mattered more than winning an argument.
