# Chapter 41: The Creator Mistake

The Creator mistake killed three drones and almost killed Fabron. The old portal ring did not malfunction; it obeyed the wrong question. Havets asked it to translate mass. The older Nomad layer interpreted the field as a coherence query, reached for compatible hosts, and tried to couple through the nearest disciplined biological system. That was Fabron, standing too close because bravery and irritation had merged in him. He dropped without a sound. His pulse fractured into impossible rhythms. Lars grabbed him while Grim flooded the chamber with decoherence noise and Mira manually grounded the field through the sensor mast. For eleven seconds, Fabron saw nothing mystical. Later he described pressure diagrams, old corridors, and millions of maintenance acts linked like a nervous system. The portal shut when Vigor's remote network interrupted with a burst of ordinary logistics traffic: food manifests, pump reports, school oxygen checks. Distributed noise broke the coupling safely. Fabron woke angry that his near-death experience involved invoices. The lesson was brutal. The Creators had treated an access medium as transport infrastructure. They had made subway doors out of a telephone for minds. Black Hole would make the same mistake with blood unless stopped.
