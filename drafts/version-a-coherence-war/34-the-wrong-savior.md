# Chapter 34: The Wrong Savior

The wrong savior called himself Saint Return and wore a stolen pressure suit painted silver. He broadcast from a refinery moon, claiming Lars Odin had opened the age of hidden angels and that chosen blood must be gathered before Black Hole polluted it. Syren amplified him, Black Hole used him as bait, and Aphex wanted to erase him with a precision strike that would have made him immortal by dinner. Lars did the crueler thing. He let Vigor feed the refinery workers, let Fabron leak fraud records, let Mira prove the suit's life-support seals were counterfeit, and let Grim release the man's donor ledger to labor unions rather than news feeds. The refinery workers removed Saint Return themselves after discovering he had spent emergency oxygen funds on stage lighting. No martyrdom. No revelation. Just a con man dragged into an airlock lobby by people with overdue hazard pay. Lars watched the feed in silence. It should have felt satisfying. It did not. The myth had not died. It had learned which costumes failed. That night, a Syren splinter cell attacked a hospital moon where Jun Vale slept through the alarm because he had chosen an ordinary life and ordinary people had chosen to defend it. The defenders won. Two died. Jun still did not ask to know what he was.
