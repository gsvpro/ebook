# Chapter 36: Dead Subway

The dead Creator portal hung between two asteroids like a broken ring of black glass. Aphex records called it mineral debris. Local miners called it the Tooth. Havets approached without visible thrust, wrapped in enough fold caution to make every alarm in Lars's bones listen. Fastulv stayed nearby as ugly cover. Mira rode a remote sensor rig, refusing to let anyone else interpret stress in a structure that old. When Havets touched the ring with a low-energy field, the portal woke for fourteen seconds. Not open. Remembering open. Space inside it folded into a corridor that led nowhere current physics wanted to admit. Grim sent a drone. The drone returned with its clock late, its hull cold, and one grain of nanomaterial on its camera mast that did not belong to Creator manufacture. Black Hole scouts arrived before the second test. They had followed Aphex curiosity, not the portal. The fight was ugly in close rock. Fabron's escort cutters used mining dust as cover; Fastulv took a hit meant for the sensor rig; Havets moved once, silently, and placed a Black Hole ship inside its own debris cone. The portal shut. The evidence remained. Creator transport was a crude layer on top of something older, and the older thing had noticed the knock.
