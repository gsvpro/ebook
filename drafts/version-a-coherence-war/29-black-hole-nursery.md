# Chapter 29: Black Hole Nursery

The nursery orbited a rogue planetoid with no name, no port registration, and too many heat signatures for its declared mining mass. Havets found it by following waste proteins through a fold-shadow route. Fastulv approached as bait; Aphex escorts stayed one light-second back under Fabron's command; Mira rode in the sensor couch because she could read grown hull stress better than any tactical package. The station was not a lab. It was a factory trying to become one. Inside were tissue vats, stolen med pods, reactor heat siphons, and children of technology grown around stolen blood samples until the bodies failed. Black Hole did not understand Aerit nanotech. It understood extraction. Lars's anger did not become a migraine. It became quiet, which made Grim move closer. The rescue was not clean. Some vats could be stabilized. Some had to be shut down because consciousness had never formed. Mira made those calls with shaking hands and exact criteria. Fabron's soldiers carried survivors who might never know why they had been made. Havets erased the station's outgoing archives but preserved evidence in distributed custody. Lars wanted to burn the planetoid. Valdrun said no. Evidence first. Models second. War third. Lars obeyed, and hated that obedience was sometimes the only civilized thing left.
