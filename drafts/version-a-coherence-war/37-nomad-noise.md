# Chapter 37: Nomad Noise

Grim called it noise because calling it speech would have started three arguments and one religion. The recovered grain from the portal did not transmit. It correlated. When cooled, heated, isolated, irradiated, or insulted by Lars, it changed state in ways that matched no local cause but paralleled fluctuations in Mira's nanotech scan and old Pluto residue. Not a message. Not a command. A medium responding to compatible structure. Valdrun compared it to hearing pressure changes through stone. Ödesvaka produced a log entry so dry it became funny: unknown field exhibits preference for coherent distributed systems and poor tolerance for centralized queries. Fabron folded his arms. 'Even physics dislikes empires.' Mira slept badly beside the lab because she refused to leave the experiment alone. Lars found her at 0300 watching a graph breathe. She asked if the word Nomad was evidence or poetry. Lars said Lyra had used it as a provisional label after eliminating seventeen worse names. The grain pulsed once when he said Lyra. No one claimed meaning. They logged temperature, field strength, and emotional context because good science recorded embarrassing variables. The model that survived said the Nomads had built routers, not temples; hosts, not worshippers; a way for consciousness-patterns to couple locally without traveling like ships.
