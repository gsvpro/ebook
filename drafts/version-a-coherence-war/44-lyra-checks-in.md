# Chapter 44: Lyra Checks In

Lyra appeared in Mira's locked lab without opening the door. The cameras showed thermal bloom, field noise, mass entering from nowhere honest, and a body assembling badly but better than the old journal version. She wore a gray work jacket, one glove, and the expression of someone arriving late to an argument she had caused centuries earlier. Mira did not kneel, faint, or accept destiny. She pointed a scanner at Lyra's chest and said, 'Consent?' Lyra smiled. 'Good.' The temporary body lasted six minutes. She gave no grand answers. She confirmed Black Hole was preparing a mass acquisition event, warned that Aphex centralization would make the harvest easier, and left a set of coordinates encoded as reactor maintenance tolerances. Lars arrived at minute four and stopped dead. No migraine. No lock. Just grief so sharp he had to put one hand on the table. Lyra looked at him with impossible fondness and professional cruelty. 'You are doing better than your face suggests.' He laughed because otherwise he would break. Fabron demanded chain of custody. Lyra handed him a residue vial. 'Human bridge,' she said. 'Do not let him become king.' Then the body destabilized into gray dust, heat, and missing mass. Mira caught every reading. Evidence first. Wonder second. Answers later.
