# Chapter 30: The Consent Problem

The second hidden Aerit was not grateful. His name was Jun Vale, he ran pressure systems on a hospital moon, and he wanted nothing to do with ancient wars, alien ancestry, locked memories, or Lars Odin's face. Black Hole had him on a market list. Syren had already sent him messages calling him a sleeping saint. Aphex wanted protective custody. Jun wanted everyone to leave. Consent made the correct answer painful. Lars showed evidence, stopped when Jun said stop, and left a hardened contact wafer on the table. Fabron objected in private with unusual heat: leaving Jun exposed felt like negligence. Mira defended Jun's refusal with equal force. 'A rescue that steals the life being rescued is still theft.' Vigor solved what doctrine could not by putting ordinary help around him: hospital spare parts, anonymous food credit, a maintenance guild rotation, and two Aphex veterans officially assigned to reactor compliance three corridors away. Jun remained locked. He also remained watched by people he had chosen for ordinary reasons. When Black Hole's scout came, it found no hero, no shrine, no central guard post. It found a hospital full of people who knew their doors, their tools, and their neighbors. The scout left bleeding coolant into a laundry drain.
