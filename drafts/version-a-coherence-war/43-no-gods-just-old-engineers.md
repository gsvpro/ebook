# Chapter 43: No Gods, Just Old Engineers

Lars lost patience with awe on a station where Syren graffiti had already turned the Nomads into angels. He stood before a wall of stolen symbols and scraped paint away with a maintenance blade while a local crew watched. 'No gods,' he said. 'Just old engineers with better tools and worse documentation.' Mira objected that this was not scientifically precise. Lars said it was socially necessary. The crew laughed, which helped more than reverence would have. Then Black Hole hit the station's outer dock. The fight became close, dirty, and unpoetic. Fabron led civilians through service tunnels marked by Clear Hand symbols no one had admitted painting. Vigor's people rerouted oxygen. Grim used a catering robot swarm to blind boarders with soup steam and cleaning acid. Lars fought in the loading bay with the Ragnarrök dialed low enough not to breach the hull and high enough to make pirates reconsider career choices. Afterward, a child asked whether Lars was one of the clear ones. He crouched, sore and bloody, and handed her a broken valve. 'Today you are. Hold this until the tech comes back.' She did. The myth bent, just a little, toward work.
