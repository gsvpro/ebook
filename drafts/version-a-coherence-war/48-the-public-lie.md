# Chapter 48: The Public Lie

The public story blamed a bio-criminal cartel trafficking illegal regenerative therapies through vaccine routes. It was true enough to stand in court and false enough to keep hidden Aerits from becoming a hunting category. Lars hated it. Mira hated it more because every omitted fact looked like a crack sealed behind paint. Fabron reminded them that civilians did not need cosmic history to prosecute murder. Arsenault wanted Aphex named as primary savior. Lars threatened to release procurement failures unless the press packet credited local customs inspectors, farm logistics, NUKEA recall teams, and Aphex escorts in that order. The chairman called him unbearable. The sector called the operation a multi-agency success. Syren tried to claim suppression of divine truth, but without names, bodies, or martyr footage, their sermon had poor traction outside people already determined to be wrong. Jun Vale sent one message: Thank you for lying correctly. Mira kept it longer than she admitted. That night Lars watched the news turn horror into digestible categories and wondered how many lies civilization required to remain kind enough for truth later. Valdrun said old wars had been lost by people who needed recognition. Lars said old guardians were annoying. Both statements were accurate.
