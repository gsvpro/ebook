# Chapter 39: The Empty Place Is Listening

KBC Void data should have been boring. Empty regions were useful because they were empty: low traffic, low radiation, low politics. Arkology Zero sat in one because Lyra had liked quiet and because enemies found nothing dramatic to target. Grim proved the quiet was structured. Background correlations dropped too cleanly. Dust distributions avoided certain harmonics. Old Creator portals bent around the region as if respecting a pressure zone. Mira built a cosmological model and then destroyed it herself because it lied politely. The KBC Void was not empty. It was low-observation by design or by long habit, a place where complex systems reduced their visible edges. 'A conservation buffer,' she said. 'Or a room where everyone agrees not to shout.' Fabron disliked the second metaphor until Lars pointed out it was operationally better than most committee language. Black Hole pressure rose while they studied. Three markets went dark. Two hidden-person leads vanished. Syren began preaching the listening emptiness as judgment. Lars recorded a public statement about sensor calibration errors and private instructions for more distributed shielding. He would not hand the galaxy a cosmic church. The data was strange enough. It did not need candles.
