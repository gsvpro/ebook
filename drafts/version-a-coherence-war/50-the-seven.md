# Chapter 50: The Seven

The seven guardians did not assemble in a noble circle. One appeared as a voice through coolant pipes. One refused visual because her local system had children nearby. One transmitted only math. Valdrun looked exhausted, Ödesvaka sounded offended by everyone, and Lars arrived late with smoke on his coat. That was probably tradition now. They agreed on almost nothing easily. Sera wanted offensive strikes against every known Black Hole biological site. The ice-archive guardian wanted centuries of patience. Lars wanted to punch the problem until it became logistics. Mira, again outside the old hierarchy, asked what outcome would remain survivable in three hundred years. The question embarrassed them into coherence. They chose limited activation, distributed evidence release, and a refusal to let Aphex, Arkology Zero, or any guardian become central command. Fabron presented the human network map: orders, guilds, farms, reactor schools, mutual-aid crews, old station rituals. Not subjects. Partners. The math-only guardian sent a pattern that translated, roughly, as approval with severe reservations. Lars answered the old way. 'Coherence?' Across seven channels came, uneven but real, 'Coherence.' Then alarms announced Black Hole's mass acquisition event had begun near the brown dwarf chain.
