# Chapter 38: Lyra's Bad Body

Lyra's first temporary body fell over immediately.

That was how Lars knew the holojournal was authentic. Lyra Vargstjärna would absolutely preserve her own humiliation if it served a technical point and annoyed future readers.

The recording came from a sealed cache inside Havets, unlocked after the Nomad noise experiment. It was late Heliostat era, long before mature Återviens had a name. The file header was not grand. It read: EMBODIMENT TEST 01 / DO NOT SHOW LARS UNLESS DEAD, MISSING, OR NEEDING HUMILITY.

Lyra appeared in a lab with too much cable, not enough sleep, and the expression of a woman about to do something historic and poorly supervised.

Her real body sat in a medical cradle behind glass.

The temporary body stood three meters away, grown from preserved tissue, nanobot scaffolding, and a field-coupling matrix that looked primitive only because Lyra had invented half the primitives herself. Cables ran from floor ports to a spine brace. A basin under one arm had already been labeled SILVER WASTE, which was either confidence or bleak experience.

Its skin held for twelve seconds before the left shoulder began shedding heat. The thermal display climbed and fell at the same time in a way that made present-day Grim pause the recording and make a small offended sound.

"Heat debt," he said.

"Later," Lars said.

The body opened its eyes.

"If this works," Lyra said through the temporary mouth, "I become unbearable. If it fails, label the left knee first."

Then it took one step and collapsed face-first into a foam mat.

The noise was not dignified. The body made a wet slap, a squeak from the mat, and then a small pneumatic sigh from one knee joint surrendering.

Present-day Grim replayed the fall twice before Lars threatened violence.

"For gait analysis."

"For comedy, you chrome goblin."

"Multifunctional data. Also, her right ankle exhibits unacceptable variance."

The bad body lasted seven minutes.

It spoke with a half-second delay, so Lyra kept interrupting herself and then insulting the timing. It tried to sit on a stool, missed by four centimeters, corrected beautifully, and slid off the other side. It saluted the wrong camera. Its left hand dissolved when Lyra tried to grip a tool, leaving three fingers on the workbench arranged like evidence in an embarrassing trial.

"Note," Lyra said, looking down at the missing fingers. "Fine motor control remains aspirational. Pride remains stupid."

It reported sensory overlap with the preserved body, then interference from something she called the older layer. The medical cradle showed Lyra's real pulse accelerating whenever the temporary body touched metal. The field matrix showed mass moving in and out of local accounting by grams, never enough to build a miracle, always enough to ruin a clean paper.

On a lab board she crossed out spirit, astral, apparition, and wrote:

FIELD-COUPLED REMOTE EMBODIMENT. LOCAL SUBSTRATE FAILURE. DO NOT LET POETS NEAR THIS.

Mira laughed once, then covered her mouth.

Lyra looked into the camera. "If you see this, Lars, fix your face. I did not vanish into magic. I built terrible prototypes, improved them, broke three labs, and learned the Pluto artifact was not primarily transport. It was access etiquette. The Creators found similar systems and built doors around them. We are stupider because we should have inherited their mistakes and instead manufactured fresh ones."

Lars had to sit down.

The recording was not reunion. It was worse: Lyra alive in method, absent in body, refusing comfort with the old efficiency.

He had wanted, shamefully and stupidly, one clean sentence. I survived. I missed you. I knew you would come. Instead he got a bad knee, a thermal ledger, and Lyra telling him not to be impressed by the thing that had nearly broken her.

That was also her. It hurt more because it was real.

She explained limits. A preserved body could anchor identity. A temporary body could host local action for minutes or hours if field conditions were quiet and substrate remained coherent. Residue stayed unstable because the matter had never fully belonged to local causality. Missing access logs, thermal anomalies, mass-balance errors: expected. Apparitions were engineering artifacts with bad vocabulary.

"Do not call this immortality," Lyra said. "Immortality is what frightened people call maintenance when they want guarantees. There is continuity, error correction, consent, heat debt, and loss."

Valdrun wept without hiding it.

Mira watched the bad body try to stand again and fail more gracefully. The temporary Lyra got one knee under itself, lifted an arm for balance, and immediately lost two centimeters of left ear into glittering dust.

"Acceptable," recorded Lyra said. "Ear not load-bearing."

Mira's mouth twisted. "She was alone."

"Often," Lars said.

"By choice?"

"Too often."

The journal ended with warning.

"The older engineers are not rescuers. They may not think in individuals the way we do. But the layer favors coherence without domination. Build carefully. If I am gone when you find this, do not centralize recovery around missing me."

For half a second another figure stood behind Lyra in the recording. Tall, indistinct, failing at the edges. Not human. Not Creator.

Then it vanished, leaving silver dust on the floor and a cold patch on the thermal map exactly where its feet had not quite been.

"Nomad?" Fabron asked, already unhappy with the term.

"Maybe," Mira said. "Maybe a manifestation agent. Maybe recording artifact. The dust is evidence. The silhouette is not."

Grim isolated the dust frame by frame. The particles decayed in a half-life matching no known nanomaterial except the residue from Door Nine and the probe. The silhouette remained useless. Evidence around a shape did not make the shape true.

Lars stared at the dust.

No gods, he told himself.

Wonder stood there anyway, measurable and enormous.

The cache produced one route key to the KBC quiet basin around Arkology Zero and a note.

If someone tries to make you central, Lars, be rude.

Under it, in smaller handwriting, Lyra had added:

Also label the left knee first. I was right about that.

Grim began to replay the fall a third time.

Lars threw a ration bar at his head. Grim caught it, inspected the wrapper, and said, "Edible emotional regulation. Efficient."
