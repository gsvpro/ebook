# Chapter 27: No Single Heart

The dead platform wanted a central heart.

That was what the Creator buoy translated badly through stolen voices, old maintenance codes, and a pressure in Fastulv's hull that made bolts complain. The platform had been built as a relay switch for something larger. It had lost its network six hundred thousand years ago and, like many lonely machines, had concluded that the solution was authority.

Grim displayed the model. "If restored, the platform will attempt to become a routing hub for all local portal-adjacent signals."

Fabron frowned. "That sounds useful."

"Centralized useful," Lars said.

"Ah," Fabron said, which proved the man was learning in the most irritating way possible.

The platform held data they needed: Creator route maps, anomaly logs, possibly the first clear evidence connecting the KBC Void to older infrastructure. It also held a failure mode large enough to invite every predator in the sector. If Lars restored the core, they could learn fast. If he let it fail, they might lose the only map they had.

Valdrun's voice arrived from Havets. "A central heart can be protected."

Ödesvaka answered before Lars could. "A central heart can be captured. Logged objection: obvious."

"I am aware," Valdrun said.

The old guardian sounded tired. Centuries did that. So did watching your people sleep while threats learned new names.

Mira, remote but fully present in the work, rotated the platform's stress model. "The core is not dead. It is conserving itself. We can ask for local fragments without waking hub behavior."

"Ask how?" Fabron said.

She hesitated. "Maintenance. Not command."

Vigor's crate suddenly seemed less ridiculous.

Fastulv docked with the platform's outer ring. Lars and Fabron crossed in suits while Grim occupied a spider drone because his android body disliked vacuum dust in joints it had chosen for theatrical squeaking. The corridor beyond the airlock held no bodies, no dramatic inscriptions, no alien cathedral. Just old composite, microfractures, frozen lubricant, and a maintenance panel worn by manipulators shaped for hands close enough to human to be unsettling.

"Creators?" Fabron whispered.

"Portal builders," Lars said. "Maybe excellent plumbers. Do not worship plumbers until they invoice you."

They found the core chamber behind a door that opened only after Lars refused three authority prompts and offered a diagnostic patch instead. Mira guided the patch from L-Prime. Vigor's emergency cultures, absurdly, mattered: the platform used biological polymer reservoirs as part of its thermal regulation. The cultures were not correct, but they were honest feedstock.

The platform accepted them.

Lights came on one at a time, local only.

A map unfolded in the chamber air. Not holographic in the modern sense. A field interference pattern made dust, frost, and suit telemetry agree to lie usefully.

Stars appeared. Then absences. Then routes that did not connect stars so much as low-noise regions between decisions.

The KBC Void glowed not as a destination but as a quiet basin.

Grim whispered over the channel, "That is not transport topology alone."

"No," Mira said. "It is communication topology wearing transport scars."

The platform offered more. A central restore prompt pulsed at the map's heart.

Valdrun said nothing.

Fabron looked at Lars. "If we wake the hub, can we defend it?"

"For how long?"

"I do not know."

"Then no."

Lars took the local fragment, copied it redundantly into Fastulv, Havets, Mira's isolated vault, Vigor's kitchen freezer network, and an Aphex legal archive disguised as procurement nonsense. The platform protested. Not emotionally. Structurally. It had been made by beings who believed routes should converge.

"No single heart," Lars told it.

The chamber lights dimmed, then steadied.

The platform did not die. It became smaller. Or perhaps it remembered how.

As they withdrew, Fabron ran one gloved hand over the old wall.

"My order says refuse the throne," he said quietly. "I thought it meant political power."

"It does. Also technical power. Also the nice chair in conference rooms."

"You are incapable of allowing gravity into a moment."

"Gravity causes fractures. Ask Mira."

Mira's voice entered dryly. "Do not cite me in your emotional avoidance."

For one second everyone laughed, even Valdrun.

Then Havets detected a ship entering silent drift beyond the platform. Not Black Hole. Not Aphex. Not NUKEA.

It carried a Syren transponder and a Creator map fragment Lars had not copied.

Someone else had chosen a heart.
