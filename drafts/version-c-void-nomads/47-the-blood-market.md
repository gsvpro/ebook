# Chapter 47: The Blood Market

Black Hole's blood market hid inside a refueling swarm that serviced ships too poor to ask questions.

Hundreds of tanks, tugs, machine shops, dorm pods, brothels, clinics, noodle stalls, prayer capsules, and illegal labs orbited a dim red dwarf under no single jurisdiction. It was the perfect place to buy fuel, organs, forged papers, reactor parts, and bad ideas.

Lars went in with Fabron, Grim, and twelve people who did not look like a team because Vigor had dressed them.

"You look like smugglers with digestive problems," Fabron said.

"Exactly," Vigor replied over comm. "No one bothers such people unless they owe money."

The market's Black Hole cell was not the main crisis, but it fed the pod fleet. Biological processor gel, stolen blood, nanobot-contaminated tissue, false consent tags, sedatives, quiet-basin navigation rumors. Cut the supply, reduce the force at First Door.

They could not simply raid it. Too many civilians. Too many hostages. Too many records that would vanish if frightened.

So they bought an appointment.

The broker wore a child's face by choice, which told Lars everything necessary about its ethics. It offered samples in sealed vials, each labeled by anomaly class. One label made Grim's entire body still.

AERIT-PROBABLE: SOVAN LINEAGE MATCH.

Mira, listening from L-Prime, said nothing.

Lars did not move for one breath.

Then he smiled like a drunk idiot.

"Expensive?" he asked.

The broker relaxed, mistaking rage for greed. "Very. The source is protected, but we have echoes."

"Echoes?"

"Relatives, discarded tissue, medical waste, pattern-compatible donors. Enough for ritual, science, or appetite."

Fabron's hand twitched toward his weapon.

Lars bought the entire lot with Arsenault's emergency account.

Arsenault, watching the transaction, whispered, "That is a painful number."

"Grow as a person," Grim said.

The purchase opened the broker's trust path. Grim followed payment metadata into the market's archive. Fabron's disguised teams blocked exits with fuel safety inspections. Vigor's people started three kitchen fires in exactly the wrong places. Rauk's dampers cut power to lab freezers but preserved civilian life support.

Then Lars stopped pretending.

The Ragnarrök remained holstered. He used hands, walls, foam charges, and the market's own cramped geometry. Black Hole soldiers liked fear and open lanes. The swarm offered neither. Civilians slammed shutters. Mechanics dropped tools into pursuers' paths. Noodle vendors overturned boiling stock with heroic profanity.

The child-faced broker tried to trigger a dead-man purge.

Mira stopped it.

Not through memory. Through materials. She had studied the market's cheap cryo seals from the purchase scans and found every stress cheat. One command sent temperature cycling through the archive racks just wrong enough to jam purge valves without rupturing samples.

"Cracks do not lie," she said, voice cold.

They captured vials, ledgers, buyers, and one living technician who had joined Black Hole after Syren convinced him the Void wanted flesh returned to the field. He cried when Vigor's medic treated his broken arm.

"Do not forgive him for needing bandages," Fabron warned.

"I don't," Lars said. "I just like civilization."

The Sovan-labeled samples were not Mira's blood. They were attempts to approximate her pattern from stolen hospital metadata and distant relatives, none close enough to explain the label. Black Hole was guessing, but its guesses were improving.

Mira requested the sample vault be destroyed.

Grim hesitated. "It contains evidence."

"Copy the records. Destroy the biological material."

Lars backed her before anyone could argue. Consent did not end when tissue left the body. They copied the ledgers, sterilized the samples, and marked the act as evidence destruction justified by personhood risk. Fabron wrote the legal note with grim satisfaction.

The raid reduced Black Hole's supply by thirty percent.

Not enough.

As they fled the refueling swarm, one buyer packet decoded. It contained final instructions for the pod fleet at First Door.

Converge through blood.
Force the quiet to answer.
If answer resists, brighten until rupture.

Lars looked toward the dark route ahead.

"They really do think the universe owes them a scream."

Grim's voice was low. "Then we deny them acoustics."
