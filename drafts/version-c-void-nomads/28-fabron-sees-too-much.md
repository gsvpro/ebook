# Chapter 28: Fabron Sees Too Much

Fabron began keeping two notebooks.

The first was official, encrypted, formatted, and written in language suitable for Aphex command review. It contained hostile movements, audit findings, tactical risks, and recommendations phrased so that cowards could approve them without noticing they were brave.

The second was paper.

In it he wrote the impossible things.

Lars Odin recognized Creator prompts without training. Grim negotiated with systems at speeds no service automaton, military AI, or illegal synthetic cognition should manage. Havets Sista Sång appeared in no sensor record yet altered outcomes. Dr. Sovan refused memory restoration but solved ancient maintenance interfaces by treating them like tired machinery. Vigor's kitchen network routed secure packets through recipes. The old phrase from Fabron's childhood matched operational doctrine older than France, Aphex, and possibly humanity's confidence in itself.

Repair before worship.

He wrote it at the top of a page and stared until the ink dried.

The confrontation happened aboard Fastulv because Lars believed difficult conversations improved when the furniture was ugly.

"You are hiding a civilization," Fabron said.

Lars leaned against a cargo crate. "That is a large accusation."

"You are not denying it."

"I deny your phrasing."

"Fine. You are hiding a people, ships, technologies, an old war, and a doctrine that my family has apparently been reciting at dinner for centuries without proper footnotes."

Grim, nearby, raised one metal finger. "Footnotes would have been tactically risky."

"I was not asking you."

"People often fail to ask the best available intelligence."

Fabron turned to Lars. "What are you?"

Lars hated the question less than he expected. Fabron was not asking as a zealot or a collector. He was asking as an officer who had realized the battlefield extended under his childhood prayers.

"Aerit," Lars said. "Old human-adjacent, not exactly human anymore, born from Pluto artifact contact and a century of survival choices. Not gods. Not rulers. Mostly idiots who lived too long and learned maintenance."

Fabron absorbed that with admirable lack of screaming.

"And Sovan?"

"Probably. By consent, she decides how much that matters."

"How many?"

"Locked? Unknown. Enough to be hunted. Not enough to save anyone by central command."

Fabron opened his paper notebook. "My order. Clear Hand. Do you know it?"

Lars read the emblem: three circles, deliberate crack. Memory stirred. Heliostat corridors. Kalari technicians learning human rituals. Lyra arguing that culture outlived encryption if it could survive being misunderstood.

"Not your branch," Lars said. "But I know the ancestor pattern."

Fabron sat down because he had the sense to do so before history knocked him over.

"We thought it was a discipline founded after the Rakh'Na wars," he said. "A lay order. Maintenance, mutual aid, refusal of imperial cults. My grandmother said the Clear Ones had saved cities by teaching people to distrust thrones. I assumed metaphor."

"Metaphor is how truth gets through customs."

Fabron laughed once, bitter and amazed. "Do you understand what this does to loyalty?"

"Yes."

"No, you understand being old and guilty. I mean my loyalty. Aphex pays me. France trained me. My order shaped me. You are now telling me the order preserves fragments of your people's survival doctrine."

"I am telling you that your loyalty is still yours."

Fabron looked up sharply.

Lars continued, "If Återviens requires you to obey me, it has failed. If it requires you to protect Aphex from becoming a cancer, protect civilians from predators, and tell me when I am acting like a throne-shaped idiot, then it may be useful."

Grim nodded. "You are well qualified for the final item."

"Thank you," Fabron said automatically, then regretted it.

The Syren ship complicated everything by broadcasting a public claim ten minutes later.

They had recovered a Creator fragment. They invited Aphex, NUKEA, independent colonies, and all seekers of the Void to witness "the first holy silence" at a neutral parley.

Mira called immediately. "They are using my stolen waveform in the invitation header."

Lars went cold.

The Syren had copied the dead platform's trick and made it theatrical. Not enough to unlock Mira. Enough to make anyone hunting her hear a scent.

Fabron closed his official notebook.

"Orders?" Lars asked.

Fabron stood.

"My Aphex order is to attend, gather intelligence, and prevent hostile monopoly. My Clear Hand order is to keep the hinge small. My personal order is to make sure nobody uses Dr. Sovan's voice again without consequence."

Lars smiled without humor.

"Coherence?" he asked.

Fabron did not know the old answer by memory. He answered anyway.

"Coherence pending."
