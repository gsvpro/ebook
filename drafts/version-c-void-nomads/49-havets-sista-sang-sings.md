# Chapter 49: Havets Sista Sång Sings

Havets Sista Sång did not sing in sound.

It sang in permission refused.

At First Door, the old Creator station floated in darkness like a wound held open by architecture. Around it came Black Hole pods by the hundreds, each carrying stolen tissue, biological processors, and enough desperate computation to force a bad question. Farther out, Syren's core ships held formation for synchronized silence. Aphex cutters and NUKEA power vessels lurked at jurisdictional edges, every captain waiting for someone else to make responsibility easier.

Fastulv arrived visible, loud, insulting.

Lars opened a public channel. "This is Captain Lars Odin of Fastulv. If you are here for transcendence, you are early, overdressed, and blocking emergency lanes."

Fabron, in the copilot chair, whispered, "That may not de-escalate."

"It de-escalates my mood."

Grim occupied the ship, Havets, and three decoy drones with the anxious joy of a distributed mind about to commit procedural vandalism.

Mira's model ran through a secure remote array. Her consent boundary included high-stress analysis and excluded identity prompts, because even at the edge of catastrophe she remained a person before she was useful. Ren Koss piloted the service route from a protected cradle, his partial memories guiding small ships through field eddies no modern nav system believed in.

Havets waited until everyone brightened.

Black Hole began the forced coupling. Pods linked through blood processors, forming one hunger across many bodies. Syren began synchronized silence, millions reduced now to thousands but still too many, breath and neural entrainment aimed through devotional relays. Aphex sensors focused. NUKEA stabilizers charged. First Door woke wrong.

Then Havets sang.

Every Black Hole pod received a local maintenance emergency. Not false. Havets found real faults: overheating pumps, contaminated growth gel, unstable processor tissue, cracked coolant loops, bad seals. It amplified each local problem until the pod's own survival routines demanded attention. The great hunger fractured into a thousand smaller panics.

Every Syren relay received unsynchronized human noise. Not jamming. Local acts. A child reciting names of dead miners. A reactor crew counting bolts. Vigor chopping onions. Madame Fabron leading a terrible hymn about batteries. Pail telling people to breathe at their own pace. Private meaning broke central waveform.

Every Aphex and NUKEA ship received liability, local hazard data, and enough contradictory but accurate safety flags that captains had to make decisions instead of obeying fleet dashboards.

Havets did not command them.

It made command insufficient.

First Door flickered.

The Creator hardware tried to become a hub. The older anchor underneath withdrew from force and opened to distributed witness. For a moment, the dark around the station filled with no light at all and yet every passive sensor recorded structure.

Lars felt it through Havets: the quiet basin accepting less brightness, not as obedience but as relief.

Black Hole adapted again. Its central predator ship, hidden behind the pod swarm, fired a biological lance toward First Door. The lance carried concentrated stolen nanobot tissue and a command pattern shaped like worship turned inside out.

"Intercept," Fabron said.

Fastulv could not. Too slow.

Havets could, but only by revealing itself in a way every sensor would see.

Lars felt the old ship ask without words.

Consent? Not from him alone. From the pattern.

He opened the distributed channel. "Havets can stop the lance, but exposure risk is high. Local votes, now."

It was absurd. Too slow by military logic. Perfect by every lesson they had learned.

Mira: yes.

Fabron: yes.

Valdrun: yes.

Ödesvaka: yes, logged under protest against drama.

Vigor: sing, old ship.

Ren: clear lane.

Grim: yes.

Lars: yes.

Havets Sista Sång became visible for 1.8 seconds.

Not as a ship. As an absence with edges, a vessel so old and strange that Aphex sensors filed it under weather before giving up. It crossed the lance's path and unfolded a local field sink. The biological command pattern lost coherence, became tissue, data, grief, waste. The lance dispersed into inert dust.

First Door dimmed.

Across the channels, millions saw only sensor failure, a flash of impossible dark, and Lars Odin laughing like a man who had just survived insulting physics.

The main Black Hole ship turned toward Fastulv.

"Now," Lars said, "we become moderately lucky."
