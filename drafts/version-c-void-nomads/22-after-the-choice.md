# Chapter 22: After the Choice

After the attack, Aphex called it a containment drill.

The lie was Fabron's idea, which annoyed Lars because it was a good one. A drill explained shutters, local control, disabled remote systems, soldiers in corridors, and Director Odin shouting at three departments through a legal channel while an elderly freighter embarrassed hostile craft near a brown dwarf.

It did not explain the black residue in the med bay, the dead infiltrator's data burst, or Dr. Mira Sovan sitting alone in a materials lab at 0300 with seventy-three years of impossible records open in front of her.

Lars found her there because she let him.

Fabron waited outside the lab door. Grim waited with him, holding two cups of station coffee and observing them with suspicion.

"This beverage is a crime," Grim said.

"Then arrest it," Fabron replied.

Inside, Mira did not look up. "You said I could stop."

"Yes."

"I am stopping before memory work."

"Yes."

"I am not stopping the evidence."

Lars took the stool across from her. He did not say Coherence. He did not reach for her mind, her lock, or the old ache behind his own restored memory. He let the data sit between them where honest things belonged.

Mira had marked the coordinate burst in six colors. "This does not point to a local rendezvous. It points to a void-aligned reference frame. Not exactly your Arkology Zero, but same family of absence. Low foreground noise. Low survey density. Low gravitational clutter."

"You found all that from one dead enemy cough?"

"The cough was verbose."

Lars smiled. "I like you."

"I am aware and reserving judgment."

She touched a different file. The old emergency footage from 2655 appeared, paused before the decompression bridge. She did not flinch this time. That cost her something. Lars could see it in the way she made her face technical.

"I do not want a full unlock," she said. "But I want to know whether the person in that video is me making choices, or something using me."

"You," Lars said. "With missing context."

"You cannot know that."

"I can know enough. The lock protects access. It does not pilot you like a puppet."

Her mouth tightened. "Convenient for both of us."

"Yes. Truth often is. That is why liars borrow its clothes."

She sat with that for a while.

When she spoke again, her voice was smaller and therefore more dangerous. "What am I called?"

"Aerit," Lars said.

The word landed without thunder. No migraine, no collapse, no mystical light. Mira's fingers twitched once on the table. The lab's acoustic sensors registered a tiny vibration from the composite rack behind her, as if something in the room had remembered a frequency.

"Aerit," she repeated. "Is that a species?"

"A people. A history. A problem. Sometimes a family if everyone involved is very tired."

"Are you one?"

"Yes."

"And Grim?"

"No. Grim is Grim, which is worse."

The door opened a centimeter. "I heard that," Grim said.

"Good," Lars said. "Improve."

The door closed.

Mira almost smiled. Then she looked at the evidence again and did not.

"If I accept partial confirmation," she said, "not memory, not identity, just a safe boundary test, what happens?"

"Grim records the boundary. Valdrun and Arkology Zero verify without Aphex. You choose one question. We answer with evidence, and if your lock pushes back, we stop."

"Valdrun. Another Aerit?"

"Yes. He is old, cranky, partially mechanical, and currently listening because privacy is complicated in emergencies."

Mira looked at the ceiling. "Valdrun, if you can hear this, I have not consented to medical or cognitive intervention."

Valdrun's voice came softly through a speaker Lars had not enabled. "Understood. Coherence is not demanded."

Mira closed her eyes.

For one breath, the room became very old.

Then she opened them. "My question is this: am I in immediate danger because of what I am, or because of what I know?"

Grim's answer appeared on the wall as three evidence branches, stripped of private content. Black Hole sampling patterns. Aphex metadata leakage. The coordinate family aligned with the KBC Void.

Lars said, "Both. But today mostly because of what someone thinks you can point toward."

Mira studied the branches for twelve minutes. The lock did not punish her. It only warmed the back of her neck and left her with tears she refused to explain.

When she finally spoke, the cost arrived.

"I cannot stay here as I was."

Lars nodded.

"But I will not go with you. Not yet."

That hurt more than he expected. Good. It meant he had not turned her into cargo in his head.

"What do you want?"

"I stay at L-Prime under Fabron's local protection. I keep my work. I get clean data, no hidden medical access, and a way to call you if evidence becomes memory without my permission. You harden this place. You do not own me."

"Agreed."

"Too fast."

"It was the correct answer."

"I dislike that too."

Outside, Fabron accepted the arrangement with a long sigh, then began writing orders that would irritate Aphex central for years. Good orders often did.

By morning, the official story spread: containment drill successful, board audit ongoing, minor hostile probe incident exaggerated by rumor.

The unofficial story spread faster through maintenance boards, reactor guilds, chapel kitchens, and soldier gossip.

Local authority held.

Repair before worship.

Somewhere beyond Aphex, someone heard it and answered with a symbol no Aphex system should have recognized: three quiet circles around an empty center.
