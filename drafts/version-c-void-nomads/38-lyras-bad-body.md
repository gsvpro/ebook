# Chapter 38: Lyra’s Bad Body

Lyra's first temporary body fell over immediately.

That was the first thing the holojournal showed, which was how Lars knew it was authentic. Lyra Vargstjärna would absolutely preserve her own humiliation if it served a technical point and annoyed future readers.

The recording came from a sealed cache inside Havets, unlocked only after the Nomad noise experiment. It was old, probably late Heliostat era, long before the mature doctrine had a name. Lyra appeared in a lab with too much cable, not enough sleep, and the expression of a woman about to do something both historic and poorly supervised.

Her real body sat in a medical cradle behind glass.

The temporary body stood three meters away, grown from preserved tissue, nanobot scaffolding, and a field-coupling matrix that looked primitive now only because Lyra had invented half the primitives herself. It opened its eyes.

"If this works," Lyra said through the temporary mouth, "I will be unbearable."

Then it took one step and collapsed face-first into a foam mat.

Present-day Grim replayed the fall twice before Lars threatened violence.

"For gait analysis," Grim said.

"For comedy."

"Multifunctional data."

The journal continued. Lyra's bad body lasted seven minutes. It spoke with a half-second delay. Its left hand dissolved when she tried to grip a tool. It reported sensory overlap with the real body, then interference from something she called the older layer.

Not spirit. Not astral. She explicitly crossed those words out on a lab board and wrote: FIELD-COUPLED REMOTE EMBODIMENT, LOCAL SUBSTRATE FAILURE, DO NOT LET POETS NEAR THIS.

Mira laughed once, then covered her mouth.

Lyra looked straight into the old camera.

"If you are seeing this, Lars, stop making that face. I did not vanish into magic. I built terrible prototypes, improved them, broke three labs, and learned that the Pluto artifact was not primarily a transport device. It was an access etiquette. The Creators found similar systems and built doors around them. We are stupider because we should have had their mistakes to learn from and instead made fresh ones."

Lars had to sit down.

The recording was not a reunion. It was worse: Lyra alive in method, absent in body, refusing comfort as efficiently as ever.

She explained early projection limits. A preserved body could anchor identity. A temporary body could host local action for minutes or hours if field conditions were quiet enough. Residue remained unstable because the substrate had never fully belonged to local causality. Missing access logs, thermal anomalies, mass-balance errors: all expected. Apparitions were engineering artifacts with bad vocabulary.

"Do not let anyone call this immortality," Lyra said. "Immortality is what frightened people call maintenance when they want guarantees. There are no guarantees. There is continuity, error correction, consent, and loss."

Fabron listened with his paper notebook closed.

Valdrun wept without hiding it.

Vigor took off his hat.

Mira watched Lyra's bad body try to stand again and fail more gracefully.

"She was alone," Mira said.

"Often," Lars replied.

"By choice?"

"Too often."

The journal ended with a warning.

"The older engineers are not rescuers. They are not gods. They may not even think in individuals the way we do. But the layer favors coherence without domination. That is enough to build with, carefully. If I am gone when you find this, do not centralize the recovery apparatus around grief. Grief loves thrones. Refuse them."

The image flickered.

For half a second, another figure stood behind Lyra in the recording. Tall, indistinct, not human, not Creator as the maps rendered them. A body made by field conditions and failing at the edges.

Then it vanished, leaving silver dust on the lab floor.

Grim froze the frame.

"Nomad?" Fabron whispered.

"Maybe," Mira said. "Maybe a manifestation agent. Maybe an artifact of the recording."

Lars stared at the dust.

No gods, he told himself.

But wonder did not care what he rejected. It stood there anyway, measurable and enormous.

The cache produced one final file: a route key to the KBC Void quiet basin around Arkology Zero.

And a note from Lyra.

If someone tries to make you central, Lars, be rude.
