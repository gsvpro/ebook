# Chapter 26: The Second Guardian Channel

The signal came through a kitchen freezer because Vigor distrusted elegant communications.

One moment Lars was staring at frozen goat. The next, frost on the back wall rearranged into seven broken circles and a line of old Aerit compression that made his restored memory lean forward like a dog hearing its name.

No migraine. No lock spike. Only an ache behind the sternum.

Valdrun joined the channel from Havets. Ödesvaka layered protocol verification around the signal, dry and severe. Grim tasted the packet's noise with quantum routines and declared it either authentic, compromised, or flirting.

"Those are not the same category," Fabron said.

"They often are in ancient systems," Grim replied.

The sender identified as Guardian Channel Two.

Not a person. A relay identity built before the collapse, associated with one of the unnamed guardians scattered after Alpha Fourteen. The content was brief.

Hold small hinges.
Creator transport layer is waking.
KBC alignment not shelter alone.
Do not gather the sleepers centrally.
Coherence?

The word sat at the end like a hand extended through centuries.

Valdrun answered first. "Coherence uncertain."

Lars hated how honest that sounded.

The channel offered coordinates for a dead survey platform near the abandoned Creator buoy. It also included a checksum only an old guardian should know and a routing path that passed through three modern civilian networks, two farm cooperatives, and one children's educational archive on Havskant.

"Distributed," Vigor said approvingly.

"Or compromised through distributed systems," Fabron replied.

Both were true. That was the irritating part about good traps.

Mira, remote on the wall, requested raw data. She had not slept much. Her silver hair was tied back, shoulder brace visible, eyes sharper than rest should allow.

"The signal timing follows local maintenance cycles," she said after a minute. "It waited for low traffic in each network, then rode harmless telemetry. That is elegant."

"Creator?" Lars asked.

"No. Too polite."

Grim brightened. "I like her."

Mira ignored him. "But there is a second carrier underneath. Field correlation. Not message-like. More like the communication path knew where low-observation gaps would be before the networks produced them."

KBC again. Quiet regions. Low survey, low noise, low attention.

Lars remembered Lyra on an old recording, laughing with cracked lips on Pluto because a piece of impossible metal had responded only when everyone stopped shouting at it.

No gods, he reminded himself. Old engineers. Old systems. Scale large enough to make theology lazy.

They argued for three hours.

Fabron wanted a military verification team. Vigor wanted three smugglers and a cook. Valdrun wanted patience. Ödesvaka wanted seventeen safeguards and said so with the air of a system that had been underappreciated for six centuries. Grim wanted to attach himself to the packet and follow it backward, which everyone vetoed so quickly he looked offended.

Lars chose the smallest hinge.

Fastulv would go visibly to the dead platform as a salvage inspection under Aphex audit cover. Havets would observe without contact. Mira would remain at L-Prime, analyzing field data without leaving the protection she had chosen. Fabron would come because he would otherwise follow. Vigor would not come because his network was more useful alive and deniable.

"No awakening," Lars said. "No gathering. No heroic reveal. We inspect a buoy and leave if it smells like religion."

"Religion does not always smell," Fabron said.

"Yours smells faintly of metal polish and disapproval."

Fabron looked pained. "That is unfortunately fair."

Before departure, Vigor handed Lars a sealed crate.

"Food?" Lars asked.

"Tools, emergency cultures, printed seedstock, and food. If you find an old system, offer it maintenance before questions."

"You think a Creator buoy wants soup?"

"I think every intelligence reveals itself by what it considers care."

At the dead platform, care looked like failure.

The platform rotated slowly in vacuum, dark except for one maintenance beacon blinking on a schedule no modern manufacturer used. The abandoned Creator buoy floated beyond it, a spindle of black material folded around empty space.

Fastulv's sensors saw mass where there was no reflection. Havets saw topology where there was no mass. Grim saw correlations that made him go quiet.

Then the dead platform transmitted in Mira's voice.

"Coherence?"

Mira, sixty light-minutes away, looked at her own waveform on the relay and whispered, "I did not send that."

Lars did not answer.

Not yet.
