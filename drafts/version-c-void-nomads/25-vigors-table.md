# Chapter 25: Vigor’s Table

Vigor did not own a throne, a headquarters, or a fleet.

He owned kitchens.

That was more dangerous.

His main hall on The Rim had once been a cargo sorting deck. Now it held long tables, hydroponic herb towers, fermentation tanks, illegal cheeses, sober smugglers, off-duty Aphex soldiers, three NUKEA technicians hiding from their supervisor, and a wall of old farm tools polished like saints' bones.

Lars arrived hungry and suspicious.

"Good," Vigor said, embracing him hard enough to threaten ribs. "Suspicion sharpens appetite."

"That sounds medically false."

"Only for cowards. Sit."

Fabron stood stiffly at the entrance. "I am on duty."

Vigor looked him up and down. "Then you will need more soup than civilians."

The soup arrived before Fabron could refuse. Grim received a bowl as well, despite not needing one. He examined it with intense reverence.

"It contains forty-seven identifiable compounds and six mysteries."

"Eat the mysteries first," Vigor advised.

The gathering looked chaotic. Lars knew better. Vigor had arranged the tables by trust network, supply route, and plausible deniability. Aphex soldiers sat near smugglers they had once arrested. Farmers sat near reactor technicians. A Syren sympathizer, thin and bright-eyed, sat where three grandmothers could watch him without appearing to watch him.

Food moved. So did information.

The old coordinate family from L-Prime matched three supply disappearances, two NUKEA recall anomalies, and one abandoned Creator-era survey buoy bought at auction by a charity front connected to the Syren Collective.

"Syren has changed tone," Vigor said. "Less anti-corporate sermon. More cosmic hunger. They say emptiness is proof of invitation."

Lars groaned. "Of course they do. The universe has one quiet room and someone wants to found a death cult in it."

The Syren sympathizer looked up. "You mock what you fear."

"I mock many things. Do not feel special."

Vigor raised a hand before Fabron could intervene. "Let him speak."

The young man, whose name was Pail, stood with the shaking courage of someone who had mistaken certainty for depth.

"The KBC Void is not absence," he said. "It is an open mouth. The old ones built a silence there so we would finally listen. Black Hole understands flesh. Aphex understands weapons. You understand nothing because you cling to repair."

The room cooled.

Lars studied him. Not hostile agent. Not yet. A lonely person handed a story large enough to crush loneliness.

"Repair is what listening looks like when you are not trying to be worshiped," Lars said.

Pail's eyes filled with angry tears. "You know more than you say."

"Yes."

"Then say it."

"No."

That hurt him. It hurt the room. It had to.

Vigor leaned forward. "Boy, when an old ship groans, you do not call it god. You check the bolts. If the bolts teach you humility, good. But you still check them."

Pail fled before dessert.

Fabron wanted him detained. Vigor refused.

"He came to be seen," Vigor said. "If we cage everyone who repeats poison, poison learns to wear cages."

"He may report," Fabron said.

"He already has, in his face, in his hunger, in the way he sat near exits. Now he will report that we fed him and did not kneel. That matters."

Lars looked at the old giant across the table. Vigor had not been one of the seven guardians in the way Valdrun was. He was food, logistics, shelter, rumor, muscle, and patience. In a century-scale conflict, that might be more useful than a warship.

After midnight, Vigor took Lars into the cold storage vault. Behind hanging slabs of lab-grown beef and real goat, a hidden screen showed a map of kitchens, farms, chapel pantries, mess halls, and fuel depots across twenty systems.

"Återviens without the word," Vigor said. "Some know a little. Most know only that when central orders become stupid, local hands keep people alive."

"Lyra seeded this?"

"Some. Maybe. Others grew because hunger is an honest teacher. Do not make it yours, Lars."

"I wasn't planning to."

Vigor snorted. "Men who accidentally become symbols rarely planned it. That is why they are dangerous."

A message arrived from Mira, clean and clipped.

The abandoned Creator buoy had awakened when exposed to the L-Prime coordinate burst.

It was not transmitting to Black Hole.

It was asking for a map.
