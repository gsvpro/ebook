# Chapter 32: The Syren Invitation

The Syren parley station had been a luxury observatory before bankruptcy, abandonment, salvage, and ideology turned it into a temple without admitting the word.

It drifted near Door Nine, an object older than human charts and younger than the artifacts it badly imitated. The station's glass cupola faced a starfield thinned by distance until darkness looked intentional.

Syren delegates wore white pressure silk and spoke softly. That was their first violence.

Their leader called herself Mother Pell, though she was thirty-two, cybernetically enhanced, and surrounded by armed converts who kept insisting they were unarmed because their weapons were devotional tools.

Lars attended with Fabron, Grim, and a visible Aphex legal observer whose terror made excellent camouflage. Fastulv docked openly. Havets did not.

Mira joined through a remote field rig, her face displayed on a portable slate she insisted Grim carry at chest height.

"Lower," she said.

Grim lowered the slate.

"Not that low. I am not a child."

"Your height preferences lack precision."

"Your arms lack diplomacy."

Lars whispered, "You two done?"

"No," they said together.

Mother Pell welcomed them beneath the cupola. Beyond the glass, Door Nine hung like a broken ring made of shadow and faint blue tension. Creator technology, crude and magnificent. Transport hardware wrapped around a field behavior it had never invented.

"We stand before the silence that birthed all roads," Pell said.

"No," Lars said. "We stand before old infrastructure in poor repair."

The Syren delegates stiffened. Fabron closed his eyes as if praying for patience or impact.

Pell smiled. "Mockery is a defense against awe."

"Sometimes it is a defense against nonsense. Awe can wait its turn."

Pell offered their evidence. To her credit, it was real. Field readings from Door Nine. The Creator fragment stolen from the dead platform. A resonance between the KBC Void coordinate family and low-observation gaps in the cosmic web. A correlation with reported consciousness projection events across centuries: visions, impossible appearances, missing mass, unstable residue.

Mira's voice sharpened. "Your correlation window is dirty. You included religious apparitions, fraud, sensor ghosts, and at least two advertising stunts."

"Yet the pattern remains," Pell said.

"A contaminated pattern can still contain signal. That does not make your interpretation clean."

Pell turned toward Lars. "And what is your interpretation, hidden one?"

There it was. Public bait. Hidden one. Not Aerit, not yet, but close enough to make the room hungry.

Lars shrugged. "That the Creators found an older communication layer, built transport around the parts they could force, and left behind machines that people keep trying to use as mirrors."

Pell's eyes brightened. "Then you admit the field carries mind."

"I admit the field carries information in ways that interact with cognition. I also admit my toilet carries water. I do not worship it."

The legal observer made a strangled sound.

Pell's smile thinned. "You reduce transcendence to plumbing."

"Plumbing saves more lives than transcendence. Ask any colony."

The trap revealed itself during the third evidence exchange. Syren had invited Black Hole not as allies but as proof of corruption. They intended a staged attack, a martyrdom broadcast, a revelation that the old field would punish predators and elevate the willing.

Black Hole, naturally, had not agreed to the script.

Their ships arrived early and fired real weapons.

The observatory shields flared. Delegates screamed. Pell looked honestly shocked, which made Lars hate her more because naive zeal could kill as thoroughly as malice.

Fabron took command of the room by shouting in three languages and shooting a devotional weapon out of a convert's hand.

"Evacuation corridors! Local teams! No remote orders!"

Mira overrode the station's field rig. "Door Nine is charging. Not from weapons fire. From the broadcast array. Syren tied their sermon into the Creator ring."

"Can it open?" Lars asked.

"Badly."

Badly was enough.

Through the cupola, Door Nine filled with blue-white stress. For a fraction of a second Lars saw not a tunnel but a mistake shaped like invitation.

Black Hole accelerated toward it.

Syren began singing.

Not mystical. Acoustic carrier, neural entrainment, field feedback through contaminated Creator hardware.

Still dangerous.

Lars drew the Ragnarrök and did not fire. Bigger weapons would make Door Nine hungrier.

He looked at Pell.

"Congratulations," he said. "You built a god out of a broken door and a microphone."

Then Havets Sista Sång entered the room without arriving.

Every light went out.
